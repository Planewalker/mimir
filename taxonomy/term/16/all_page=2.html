Modron Scope-Lens of the Horizon

Submitted by Anonymous on Thu, 2004-09-09 01:00
in
  • Nonmagical Equipment
  • Equipment

The aqua-marine lens of this scope is a drop of water from Oceanus. The drop has been frozen with a view of Elysium's skies on its surface, giving anyone who sees through it the ability to see objects at an incredible distance. It increases the wearer's ability to hit, but it reduces their ability to focus on objects that are less than 10 feet away.(This submission was converted, with permission from Wizards of the Coast, from Planescape:Torment).

Chained-Teeth Earrings

Submitted by Carnifex on Thu, 2004-09-09 01:00
in

  • Equipment

The idea of most thieves wearing anything for purely ornamental value is a suspicious one, and upon closer examination it can be seen that these thin metal earrings are actually a series of well-crafted lockpicks. If need be, you can unhook them and use them to pick open stubborn locks. They act as masterwork thieves picks, except they can be easily concealed as a normal earring.(This submission was converted, with permission from Wizards of the Coast, from Planescape:Torment).

Capture Hood

in

  • Equipment

The capture hood is a large leather sack with dozens of small pin holes that is thrown over a person's head and drawn tight around the person's waist. It effectively pins the captured person's arms to their sides. Guide lines can be tied to the bottom of the hood to drag somebody along, or restrain a particularly strong capture. The hood is favoured by the Harmonium, and slavers alike.By: Brett Rombough

Kasseg, Cerebral Symbiote

Submitted by Carnifex on Thu, 2004-09-09 01:00
in
  • Nonmagical Equipment
  • Equipment

The Kasseg are a species of mental symbiote that feed on the nervous energy of sentient creatures. Believed to be a deviant branch of the dreaded intellect devourers, the kasseg is different in that it soothes the mind and bolsters its host's higher mental processes, taking over certain mundane thinking tasks in exchange for feeding on his nervous energy for a time. By: Christopher Allen

Gehennan Spice

Submitted by Carnifex on Thu, 2004-09-09 01:00
in
  • Nonmagical Equipment
  • Equipment

Gehennan Spice is a new drug that's becoming very popular amongst Sensates. This substance is a fine orange-red powder harvested from a rare species of lower planar plant that can be found around the volcanoes of Gehenna. Recently the yugoloths have started farming the spice plants and processing them into the spice which is then sold on to buyers across the Great Ring.

Dustman's Robes

Submitted by Carnifex on Thu, 2004-09-09 01:00
in
  • Nonmagical Equipment
  • Equipment

Members of the Dustman faction often wear heavy and thick dull grey cloaks, their long robes and hood capable of keeping the wearer almost entirely covered. The thick material of the hood dulls outside noise and distractions when pulled over the head, and the rough harsh cloth slightly numbs the skin; the point of all this is to aid the Dustman to discard his emotions by first dulling his senses. By: Christopher Allen

Anarchist's Garb

Submitted by Carnifex on Thu, 2004-09-09 01:00
in
  • Nonmagical Equipment
  • Equipment

Any good Anarchist knows the value of a quick and easy disguise; when the Hardheads are on your tail, you need to be able to throw them off. For this reason many Anarchists wear jackets that, if taken off and flipped inside-out, have a different colour on the inside, or have a neckerchief hidden in a shirt pocket that can be quickly pulled up round the neck, or any number of other small tricks and trinkets that can collectively give an Anarchist a pretty good disguise if he's just given a few seconds.By: Christopher Allen

Cold Box

Submitted by Anonymous on Thu, 2004-09-09 01:00
in
  • Nonmagical Equipment
  • Equipment

This appears to be a simple, large iron box with a door on one side. Its dimensions are usually 5' by 3' by 3', but larger and smaller versions have been produced. The interior contains several metal shelves and drawers, perfect for storing food on. The Cold Box is becoming an increasingly popular item among Sigil's upper class as a means of food storage. Due to rising demand, merchants have been funding an increasing number of expeditions to the Paraelemental Plane of Ice for the necessary component, eternal ice.By: Matthew David
