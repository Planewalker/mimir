New ruling: Immediate

New ruling: Immediate Actions

Immediate actions are somewhat difficult to deal with in a forum setting.  Furthermore, there are a lot more immediate actions and reactions in 4e than there were in 3e, and ALL of them are optional.  So, I propose the following two guidelines for using immediate actions:

1.) If you have an immediate reaction that you think will be triggered, remind me that you have the ability in the OOC portion of your combat post and remind me what triggers it.  I will activate your ability at the first triggering opportunity and include it as part of my post.  This will be easier to do for some powers like Shield that change your defenses because then I won't have to go back and retroactively change the resolution of certain actions.  Some abilities such as opportunity attacks won't require this.

2.) If an immediate (re)action applied during the last DM combat post, make a note of it and express your desire to use it in your next post.  Where possible, I'll go back and adjust things the next round.  This will work best for abilities that don't actually change any roll modifiers/defenses, but instead provoke opportunity actions or reaction actions. 

Any thoughts?
