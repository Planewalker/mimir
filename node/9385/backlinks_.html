Hi all! As I've mentioned before I've been working on a new site for planewalker, something to get the bugs out of this site set and get us into something with a little more options nad power under the hood. Most importantly to me at least, the new site would give me more time to work on more important things like building our community and managing our project list!

But I'm now at that stage where I need to get some feedback. If you could take a moment to swing by the beta site at: /_drupal/ and play around I would greatly appreciate it. This is NOT the final style or appearance of the site, and there are a number of buggy elements as it is only a partial load of data from our main site. There's little need to tell me that the forum section doesn't match the style of the rest of it (I can see it)..

So, let me list for you what I would like feedback on at this moment:

1) Does the reorganization of our sections look and feel about right? I've tried to divide it along the line of fluff vs. crunch to make it easy to go for the bits you want.

2) Does article creation seem to work well for you? Is it too clunky or complicated?

3) How about the encyclopedia - is that also too clunky to work with? Wiki style freelinking, aka title+here should work in the encyclopedia - does it?

If I could get feedback on those three core elements it would be great. I plan to wipe the data out before a full install of the site, so don't worry about putting up test material.

'Zimrazim' wrote:
Will the new version of the website also include stuff like "There are currently X players online" and so on?

It can, yes.

There's lots of benefits here to moving to the Drupal platform. (And we *are* moving to it - I just can't keep maintaining the current code and fulfill my duties as a moderator and participant on site.) We just need to make sure it feels comfortable once we settle in.

Since Drupal is an open source system - we have a Lot more available features. To give you an idea of the power of this system - there are major media outlets that are using Drupal now. www.miami.com for example.

The features I've already turned on for when we move include:

Buddy lists, Blogs, Friends Pages, File upload, and the ability to have multiple Wiki's on different subjects going on the site at the same time. Plus RSS feeds and I think I saw a Twitter feed system available as well. And then there's the upgraded search system...

Also for management purposes - articles will automagically appear in the right sections, on the front page, and in any RSS feeds making article management a snap.
