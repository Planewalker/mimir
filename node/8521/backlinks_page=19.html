Daraz morphs his features and enters Void Keys. Outfitted with the inexpensive travel gear, he looks like a typical peniless githyanki refugee.

Within, the shop is full of little shelves and alcoves, each with a collection of odd knick-knacks for use as portal keys. The shop is a warren of small rooms with low archways between. At first, Daraz does not see anyone minding the shop. There is a sign, however, just inside the door, "All Keys 3 Jinx."

Daraz, does, however hear voices from another room. He peeks through the rooms and catches sight of a young gith (Mim) talking with two armored githyanki. As he gets closer he hears their conversation. Mim is telling one of the githyanki about the state of affairs in Git'riban, Sigil, and the planes. The other armored warrior interjects from time to time, both with statements affirming or expounding, and with questions of his own for Mim. It seems that the first warrior has but recently arrived in Sigil, while the second is a resident of Git'riban, but evidently a fairly new one.

Daraz catches sight of the speakers, while remaining hidden himself.

Mim is a young adult githyanki with a shaved head save for a single braid of silver-white hair (a very, very rare color for githyanki). The two warriors have gear with similar design accents, indicating they hail from the same warband. Their belts and straps are tan, with bronze buckles, and their armor incorporates bronze accents. Daraz recognizes the pair, both by their armor and their voices, as being the two githyanki who were in a heated arguement with Gy-Nath in Haven Port Inn, just before the party made his aquaintance.

Void Keys

Daraz remains undetected by Mim and the pair of Bronze warriors. He notices that the warrior that seems new to Git'riban is wearing lighter armor, leather studded with bronze plates. By their conversation, it seems that, not only is he new to Sigil, but knows little about the incursions and the fall of Vlaakith. Daraz recognizes the other one from the streets of Git'riban. He has lived in the ghetto for at least the past month or so.

"Would you like a portal to the Astral? They shift greatly, but my master keeps timetables and maps..."

"No. I need more information, brother." The newcomer interrupts Mim. He nods to the other warrior who offers more jink. "Have you seen another warrior of the Bronze? A female warrior?"

"I'm sorry, honorable warrior, but though we sell information, I cannot talk about a client. Such are the orders of my master. However, rest assured that you yourselves are now clients as well."

The newcomer gets angry. Very angry. Suddenly flying off the handle, he swears and calls Mim a Zerth-lover and coward. For a moment he looks like he may draw steel, but the other warrior restrains him, and guides him toward the door.

Their route toward the door does not take them past Daraz, and he manages to remain hidden. The warrior is still swearing loudly as he is pushed out the door by his band-brother. The pair pass Ya'shenn on the stoop.

She enters the shop and looks around. She quickly lowers the bar, locking the door, and walks toward the back of the shop, intercepting Mim, and keeping him from walking into the room where Daraz hides.

"Welcome to Void Keys, sister. Is there someplace you'd like to go, or something you'd like to know?" Mim asks when he he sees her.

Ar'dru

As Ar'dru nears the ground, he quickly realizes that he cannot pick out the particular arch that led here from Sigil. His rate of speed is high, but the three abashai are right on his heels. When he reaches the hilltops, he dodges and weaves around the ruins, looking for a familiar landmark.

As Ar'dru passes yet another unfamiliar ruin, a net suddenly flies up, and ensnares one of the abashai! Half a dozen fiendish kobolds erupt from hiding. They fling sling stones at the two other pursuing abashai. Ardru sees, partway down the mass of rock, is a small cave. A kobold wearing robes shows himself in the cave mouth and conjures a burst of flame to get Ar'dru's attention, before ducking back in.

Ar'dru

Ar'dru follows the fiendish kobolds into the tunnels. The spaces are tight, usually about 5' square. There are about half a dozen kobolds traveling with him, dragging the headless corpse of the black abashai Ar'dru slew.

[DM - Did Ar'dru collect the head?]

The sorcerer guiding him stops often to disarm or circumvent many traps and pitfalls along the way. The tunnels are a maze. Some are filled with smoke. Some narrow to the point where Ar'dru must remove his armor to squeeze through.

[DM assumes Ar'dru re-dons his armor after these points]

Eventually, they enter a domed cavern of worked stone blocks. The chamber is not large, a round floorspace perhaps twenty feet in diameter, but other caves open up on other levels. There are no balconies or ledges, but the blocks provide many handholds for the kobolds to climb up. The chamber is fairly crowded with kobolds. As soon as those kobolds accompanying Ar'dru throw the abashai's headless body on the floor, they and several others there set to devouring it, skin, meat, bone and marrow.

As brutal as the feeding frenzy is, it pales in comparison to the one which ensues when the live abashai caught in the net is brought in a few minutes later.

Only about half of the kobolds eat, however, and the foul, poisonous meat soon incpacitates those that do with wracking stomach pains. Still, from the wastral, emaciated look of these fiendish dire kobolds, and the envious looks they gave the eaters, Ar'dru realizes they are the lucky ones.

Ar'dru feels dozens of hungry eyes upon him, but none of the kobolds makes any moves to attack him. The very first of the kobolds to feast was a a slightly larger one covered in what looks like dried blood. A few other kobolds aid him, as the poisonous flesh of the abashai has him doubled over in pain. The sorcerer that led Ar'dru here did not eat. He speaks with the blooded one before returning to Ar'dru's side.

"The Scion of Kurtulmak will meet with you shortly."

Several kobolds carry the Scion over to Ar'dru.
