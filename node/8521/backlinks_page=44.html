Kirath's edrich blast

Kirath's edrich blast engulfs one of the soldiers, the darker, leathery-looking one.  The Yuan-ti screams and starts rolling around on the ground.

K'tch stands and runs forward, throwing knives.  The first hits the other Yuan-ti in the chest, just above the armored plate.  It sticks there, and the soldier grasps at it and yanks it out. The yuan-ti stands back up, and wincing in pain hefts then throws his spear at  K'tch running towards him.  The bard dodges at the last second, recieving a tiny, bleeding cut on his cheek. He draws a shortsword, and the singed Yuan-ti draws a wavy-bladed sword.

======

The sound of armored githyanki boots resounds down the corridor.  The Yuan-ti facing K'tch widens its reptilian eyes and shouts something in its own language.  Three more guards hustle down the path from behind the foliage.  They are supported by a pair of slaves bearing tower shields to protect their inhuman masters.

[DM- will keep init order for rest of garden battle; Gy-Nath=24, K'tch=21, Ya'shenn=18, Kirath=14, Ii'jyka'vaar=12, Du'minh=11, All Guards=9, Ar'dru=5, all illithids=? (will roll when you see one)]

K'tch starts singing a tune as he stalks around the guard and the two fence.  The tune is unfamiliar to anyone who hasn't been a resident of Git'riban for a goodly amount of time, as it is in fact a gith pirate song.

Slave driver ... the table has turned

Catch a fire ... ye gonna get burned

Catch-a-fire

Every time I hear the crack of a whip

Me blood runs cold

We remember on the squid ships

They brutalized our very souls

Now we *know* that we are free

Though not from poverty

Great Gith, this illigitamacy

Ain't just a way to make money

Slave driver ... the table has turned

Catch a fire ... ye gonna get burned

Catch-a-fire

The other githyani warriors emerge into the gardens.

The burned guard manages to put himself out, and stands, aided by his spear.  He does not get all the way up before Gy-Nath rushes up and runs him through.

Ya'shenn points the force dorje at the new squad of guards coming down the path, and thinks into it.  The blast, however, is deflected by one of the shieldmen.

Kirath, hovering in the air, has a better angle of attack.  He is able to fire over the tops of the shields, and hit the soldiers behind.  He does so, releasing another eldrich blast of hellfire.  The yuan-tis' armor incorporates a leather hood, and so the soldier's armor seems to take the brunt of the damage.  One of them is burned, but not fatally, and they all continue to advange together, long spears sticking out in front of the shields.

Ii'jyka'vaar and Du'minh move to intercept these newest guards, leaving K'tch to his duel.  Du'minh strides forward and swings the arbiter.  He must get by the slaves with the shields in order to attack the yuan-ti soldiers.  He choses one, and brings the wicked flail over the top of the shield.  The strike catches the slave on the top of his unprotected skull and pops a hole in it.  The slave falls to the ground.

Ii'jyka'vaar, meanwhile, simply rushed up and kicks the shield of the other shieldman.  Already off-balance from Ya'shenn's blast, he staggers backward into his master.  Ii sees her opportunity, and uses her blade's reach to strike at one of the Yuan-ti.  The strike catches the soldier in the forehead, and it gushes blood.  The Yuan-ti falls back but does not fall down.  It clasps one hand over the wound and still tries to stab forward with its spear, but is ineffective.

The other two soldiers, however, are not ineffective.  They stab forward with their spears, which manage to find their way through the armors of both Ii and Du.

A few yards behind them, they hear K'tch's song end in a cry, as the yuan-ti proves the better fencer and slices into the githyanki bard.

Ar'dru finds that the gardens provide even less room to spread his wings than the corridor did.  He has two choices, land behind his fellows, or head upward and come back down on the guards.  He elects the later, and a few seconds after Ii pulls back off her enemy's spear, she sees the bulky form of the dithka'gith swoop down like a winged reaper from the soldier's left side, and chop the yuan'ti in half at the waist.  The sweep is so great that it also slices open a second soldier, the one Ii already wounded, and fells him too.
