I'm so pleased to meet with your approval. Ya'shenn restrains herself from saying this aloud, of course. While it's nice that the wand is no longer pointed directly at her, the psion is not inclined to relax very much.

"First, I'm aware of at least some of the story concerning how your son was discovered to be... enthralled." He would probably find out through the Rish, eventually, anyway. "It's... unusual. So much so that I would never have credited it, if the speaker hadn't believed so thoroughly in the truth of what he was saying."

At this point, the psion looks away for a long moment, as if she's clearly disturbed (and quite possibly also nauseated) by some particular thought. "It's a... smaller detail, but... I'm not sure if you're fluent in the script yourself, but I may be able to identify the specific characteristics of your son's mind-mark. If I were to... examine it further, should you wish me to. I cannot guarantee that, though. Some thought-structures remain untranslated to this day; others are merely very obscure."

"There's something I'd like to ask you, as well, Schaaz-Khaal. " She leans forward slightly in the chair. "An illithid does not... put a mark like that on Mim in passing. He had to be in the power of a ghaik for a fairly significant amount of time for that to happen." Ya'shenn wonders for a second time about Schaaz-Khaal's hair. He could attribute that to an errant fireball. "I'm sure you kept an eye on your son's activities. Was there a time when he disappeared for a while, went missing...?"

Ya'shenn takes a moment to pointedly look around the room. "I have trouble imagining it to be otherwise, really, but can I assume that you have some kind of warding against scrying, and particularly against remote viewing, in place here?"

Swimming amongst the shadows, Rey'arteb clandestinely monitors the entrance, simultaneously taking note of other personas that may be in the vicinity. For some reason, a light mist begins to cover the area. He believes the mist only adds to his camouflage, that is until a soft hand, cold yet firm grasps his arm from behind.

Coming a few inches away from a spinning unarmed counterattack, Rey stops his bony hand short of her face.

Her beautiful, bloodless face.

My love, I have returned

Rey hugs her warmly, still surprised to see her here – her body is cold, and her skin lifeless.

After a short while, she breaks the embrace.

What have you gathered?

The assassin divulges only the information gained from the Ulitharid – the locations of the hubs of the house, the portal key (an empty psi-crystal), as well as the potential weaknesses. Beyond this, Rey does not reveal a thing about Grazz, nor the plans of the party.

The mist envelops him as Ista K’ait senses there is more to the story than Rey recounts. He also notices that his feet are tingling, and he looks down to see cranium rats twirling around his leg.

Not threatened in the least, but rather, more aroused as he is enveloped by the essence of the Undead Beauty, he discloses the war band’s plan of action.

But they are not prepared as I am – they have not this gift…

My love, your intent and actions are noble, though you need my guidance. I shall watch closely over you, protect you.

After she says this, she turns around and disappears into the mist. The mist doesn’t dissipate, though, as Rey had expected. It hangs over him like burdensome cloud of dread.
