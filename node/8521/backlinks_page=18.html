“It is highly alarming with such breach in security among the ranks of the Eyes. I suppose there is the possibility that Schaaz-Khaal did indeed take precautions to learn Mim’s true allegiance but sought to play the ghaiks at their own shadow game. Still, we should expect the worst – our assassination team is currently en route to take the traitor down.”

Du’minh reaches out through the telepathic link to contact Ya’shenn.

“Take the utmost precautions with your target, sister. I have learned that Mim is a spy much deeper than what could be expected – she may have special items, allies or other aid that shields her from psionic detection.”

“I also ask that you attempt to seize any evidence of her activities – her “master” Schaaz-Khaal is an important figure in operations to which I am associated.”

Returning his attention to K’dar, the Rish-I-Chal listens carefully on his update on order of day among the Silver Eyes. His eyes widen at the news of Gith’s sword.

“So the Sword now allegedly lies in the hands of the commander of the True? That is… astounding, to say the least. I can understand the overall reason why we joined the Eyes, though I am a bit leery of the whole bloc division that is developing. On a longer term I rather hope to work for unity among the People, for the reforming of an empire that once was.”

The hunter sighs, nodding to his old friend.

“Regardless, I appreciate the openness, K’dar - let us speak more of this when time allows. May silver winds speed your way to recovery.”

Unless his captain has more to say, Du’minh departs and finds his way to the old sharpener’s shop while staying mindful of telepathic exchanges among his companions.

The actual portal is an archway back in the basement of the temple. Boz leads Ar'dru to a chapel underneath the main temple. The cobbles are bloodstained, and an altar lies before the arch, flanked by golden brazzers filled with incense. Boz, however, simply takes the dustman's corpse from the duthka'gith and dashes it uncerimoniously against the side of the archway.

The portal sputters to life, the smell of brimstone wafting through on a strong breeze.

***

The portal exits in an elaborately carved archway of black basalt. The archway stands on the top of a hill of dark stone. The landscape around is rugged, and similar hills to the one on which they stand, surround them, piling atop one another in great heaps of black stone, until they meet the great bluffs and chasms of The Mountain itself. The archway seems to be all that is left of a black tower, and similar ruins are planted like flags to decay atop most of the hills around. A few are void of all but a few wrought stones sitting atop the black rock, but a few, very few, seem to be in better repair, though no lights shine in this 'city'.

And a city it may be, for lines of smoke snake from some of the less damaged ruins, and from caves within the dark hills and gullies.

Boz crouches down as soon as they hit Avernus, and spends long moments scanning the terrain for any response to their arrival before flying quickly to another hill and laying low there for several minutes.

So it goes, they stay hidden whenever something is seen by either pair of draconic eyes, and move swiftly when the coast is clear.
