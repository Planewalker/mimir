Fiendish Females!

Hi, I'm new to Planewalker.com, but I love Planescape (mostly due to Planescape: Torment). This is my version of the infamous demon of allure and lust, the succubus! She's a little different from the standard versions, her arms and legs are covered in a scaley-sort-of substance, which I thought worked well. I wrote a little introduction for her, as if a party encounters her for the first time. I hope you like it! Any comments are welcome.

The being before you immediately takes your breath away. She looks like a humanoid of perfect form; her face is easily the most beautiful you've ever seen, with full, smouldering lips and dark, mysterious eyes that draw you to her. Her body moves in a way that accentuates every curve, while her long dark hair flows like it is constantly being russled by a soft etheral breeze. You hardly notice her clawed hands, or her scaled legs, or even the pair of dangerous yet captivating wings on her back, for on this body it all seems perfect. She smiles with fanged teeth as you approach. You know you would do anything for her if she asked it of you.

*The next fiend I'm working on is the Erinyes, which should be finished soon. Thanks for looking!

-Tiefling

As the demons have found ways to improve their kind through different means, it did not take long for the devils to catch on and develop their own advanced forms as well. Evolved from the erinyes, the brachina is lawfully evil seduction given form. Whereas the lilitu takes the primal aspects of the succubus and expands upon them, the brachina subdues her devilish nature, refining it to become a perfect form to tempt mortals to embrace evil laws and all their delicious agonies. Their sexually charged, torturing nature has given them the name of Pleasure Devils. They are not fighters (unlike the erinyes) preferring to charm and deceive their enemies to do their bidding.

Having a fondness of leather and spikes, a brachina is over 6 feet in height, not including the platforms.

I hope you like her, and thanks for all your comments on my previous drawings!

Quote:
Her vulcan eyebrows remind me of genies and Emperor Ming from Flash Gordon .
Actually I used Tony DiTerlizzi's drawing of the erinyes as inspiration for that- his version showed their seductive side before 3rdEd changed them to be more combat orientated, and I wanted to incorporate the origins a little

Quote:
She reminds me of a lady minion from PC game; "Dungeon Keeper 1&2" for some reason. She has also enjoyed to torture (and to be tortured by her master-you) enemy adventurers until they turn to dark side.
I believe you're thinking of the Mistress unit. I can see now where there are similarities, it's because I was going for that whole bondage/s&m look- I like mine more though

Quote:
Anyways: what´s next, what´s next?
I'm not sure just yet. I've had enough of fiends for now... I've been focused on uni work for a while, and now I can draw what I want to again. I currently want to sketch my D&D characters cause they've all been floating around my head for too long screaming to be given form! Also, I want to have a go at drawing some other outsiders- namely celestials.

That's not to say I wont go back to fiends- I love them too much, and still hope to show you all some more of my own versions [specifically an alu-fiend, Malcanthet, and some male fiends too!] I'll probably start a new thread for these newer drawings though, so it'll be a while before this is updated again.

Also, I want to start working with colour again... it's been ages since I last coloured my works! And I also want to get my Deviant Art gallery up and running, and I have several games waiting to be played (FF XII, God of War II, Okami...) so I'll be busy busy busy

Quote:
Don't be surprised if I beg to use her for the PSCS
I'm fine with you using her and any of my drawings for whatever you want as long as you credit me (although I have no idea what the PSCS is...). The reason I've been re-drawing fiends that already have been given form is because they can sometimes look a little bland, and I hope that my versions possibly give others inspiration and allow them to explore different avenues of representation

Quote:
- how good are you at guys?
I like to think just as good as I am with girls, the reason I've been focusing on the ladies is because I feel they can sometimes look a little too much alike, and I wanted to portray them more individually (and it gives me the excuse to draw something pretty )

Sorry for rambling on, but I hope that answers a few questions. Thanks so much for your comments though, I love good feedback
