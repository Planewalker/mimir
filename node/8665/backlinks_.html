How, exactly, do Dragons get all that loot back to their lair...?

I mean, think about it... a lot of the time, you find a dragon in its lair lying on top of a huge pile of coins (thousands and thousands at the very least), gems, assorted art objects, weapons and armor, etc... HOW in the name of Io does the dragon move all that stuff from wherever it came from BACK to its lair? Especially small items like coins that would just slip thru its claws if it tried to grab a pile... and not every dragon owns a convenient item like a bag of holding to put all that loot in! Maybe they hire humanoids to haul it in wagons? (but the dragon would have to watch them every step of the way, or they'd surely make off with it...) Of course, some dragons can polymorph into humanoids themselves, but either way it seems like one dragon by itself would have an awfully hard time taking all those goodies back to its hidey-hole...

How, exactly, do Dragons get all that loot back to their lair...

As for Planescape, I always thought having a dragon-ran bank would be an interesting twist on the dragon's hoard concept. It could definitely be a thing I could see some dragons doing, and I'd imagine the default rate would generally be low; getting eaten/breath attacked to death by a very PO'ed dragon would probably be something most people getting a loan would be very assiduous in avoiding, especially as it would probably be legal on the dragon's part, assuming he didn't hurt anyone else or anyone else's property. Plus, then they can reasonably ADVERTISE how difficult it is to successfully get money out of their hoard, and have other people pay them to make it more so. It would be a win-win situation for the dragons. Since teleports into the banks aren't likely to be easily available in Sigil, that means potential bank robbers have to physically assault the banks in order to steal the cash. (Granted, you have a chance of finding a portal, but anyone who knew about a portal into an 'unrobbable' bank would be wise to make sure the knowledge stayed dark lest they get killed or otherwise.)
