Yes, now if we could only get Trias back, or Ben ...

Now, one of the main reasons I like doing play-by-post, is that I'm always up for becoming a better writer. As such, I want my players to critique my work. Tell me, what do you like, not like, want more of, etc. Are the characters of depth? Are descriptions clear yet imaginative? Does the language flow? Is it too R-rated? How am I doing with Theme? Feel of the setting? Are the plotlines catchy? Let me know.

So, on to the next ...

If the party is going through with the raid on illithid house (and why wouldn't they?) I'm going to have to come up with the stuff. Basicly, using Grazz's knowledge, you all can go after any of the three hubs. There will be three smaller dungeons inbetween, however, as you catch connecting portals. Althogh, technicly, each of the three hubs would be connected to different minor lairs and portal-switches, and there are a variable number of portals to take in each case, I am simplyfying it for plot purposes.

I am also, however giving you a choice. I will come up with three small Undersigil lairs, give you guys some info on each, then you all decide on which ones to tackle first.

So far, my brainstorm rained out (well, snowed out actually) the following ideas: {EDIT}

The Mud Show - An area of Undersigil with alternating portals to ooze, water, and sometimes septs on the primes. The result is a series of chambers filled with 0-5 feet of water, mud, or a combinanation. The dabus have engineered the chambers to drain away, so there may not be anything, or the PCs may be up to their armpits in muck. The only perminant resident is "Mud Show" an illthid bard. Barmy as they come, it dwells apart from the others, the only brains it gets are sub-standard, old, diseased, or otherwise 'marginally edible' thralls brought from the prime septs as appeasements for the guardian, who is barmy enough to attack other ghaik from time to time.

The King and Id - This lair is acually on a prime world. The palace of a king hosts more than one portal to Sigil, and the illithid have become the power behind the throne in order to control them. Of course, they don't want some fool guard or servant to blunder into them, so certain areas of the royal palace are off-limits to all but the seldom-seen royal family, though the palace always needs new servants, cooks, and guards it seems ...

The third I haven't come up with yet. Shoot, if I come up with a fourth and fifth, I might let you choose between 'em.{ED}

{}Marching Order - Typical Undersigil lairs, all looking the same but really being separate places requiring different portal keys, which must be used in the correct sequence, by the person(s) on point. Shifting nature of the portals means that if the 'next' portal key doesn't activte a portal, you have to avoid that portal, in case one of the other keys sets it off, and you loose track of which one you're in. A 'failsafe' of a fourth portal key, when used on some other portals in the area, brings you to a random spot on the streets of Sigil, making this one a good escape route too.

{}In the Closet - Some of the 'rooms' in Illithid House are legit Sigilan businesses as cover, while behind the scenes the mind flayers benefit financially as well as devouring random customers or employees from time to time. Ideas for front businesses: busy inn, high-priced lady's ward purfumery or gem shop, hive ward brothel, soup kitchen near the Gatehouse, humanoid bar, seedy gambing den, fine silk clothing store, orphanage (damn you EvilDM).
