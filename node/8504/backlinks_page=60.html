Advantages of a level in fighter:

d10 for hitpoints
+1 to hit for melee and missile attacks
+2 to Fort saves
Proficiency with all armor/martial weapons
1 fighter bonus feat (weapon focus/longsword?)
Intimidate as a class skill

(So, +2 to hit for attacks with longsword, for example, and +1 to hit with missile weapons, but no bonus to damage. The ability to wear armor is tempting, but Ya'shenn has the Inertial Armor ability already.)

Advantages of a level in rogue:
d6 for hitpoints
+2 to Reflex saves
Average of +3.5 damage on each successful sneak attack. Ya'shenn flanks monsters constantly for the +2 attack bonus anyway.
Proficiency with light armor
More skill points than fighters, and a wide variety of skills to choose from.
Would the multiclass XP penalty be particularly significant (or for that matter, even exist) in this game?

(No bonus to attack rolls, but +3.5 or so to damage rolls for sneak attacks. It's been easy to flank monsters so far, but she would probably seldom have opportunities to sneak attack with missile weapons. Also, her sword's special bonus is versus undead, and good luck sneak attacking the undead...)

Advantages of a level in psion:

Mostly, two new powers (3rd and 4th level). Examples include Whitefire (a decent AoE attack) or Telekinetic Thrust (or Hostile Empathic Transfer) as well as powers such as Mindwipe, Charm Monster, Tailor Memory, Divination, Freedom of Movement.

Disadvantages: Whitefire and Telekinetic Thrust (for example) have significant power point costs. Power resistance mostly applies, though I would guess that using telekinetic thrust to hurl a longsword/spear/whatever at a mind flayer would not trigger power resistance.
