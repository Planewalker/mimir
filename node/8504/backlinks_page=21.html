'Zimrazim' wrote:
Actually, what is the 'proper' term to use when addressing a duthka'gith? I could have sworn I read a 'cousin' a couple of times in earlier posts, but I'm not sure. (Whatever the 'proper' term is, she'd most likely be using it.)

Proper? Most githyanki use "cousin", which is what they'd use for a gith pirate or athasian gith that deserved a little respect. Of course "brother" would be a lot more polite and inviting, but most githyanki are prejudiced against the halfbreeds, so it becomes 'cousin', and the term gets picked up even by those who have no problem with duthka'gith, entering the lexicon. The duthka'gith, on their part, accept being called 'cousin' because they hold themselves as a cut above anyway.

So, 'cousin' is the proper term, if you will, but calling Ar'dru "brother" would be an unexpectedly nice thing to do.
