'Dire Lemon' wrote:

Azure. Your NPCs all seem really interesting and stand out compared to allot of games I've seen. Does it bother you that they tend to get killed off so quickly or is that the point? I mean, what motivates you to create so many unusual characters and then pit them against the killing machines of Git'riban?

My motivation for creating NPCs is simply as a creative outlet. At that, I have litreally dozens of NPCs in my PS campaign at home who haven't shown up here (and in all likelyhood, won't).

As for their mortality vs the githyanki, well, everybody's gotta die sometime.

But really, sometimes I'm tempted to try keep 'em alive, but just as often I do enjoy watching them get killed spectacularly! Yes, I'm a bit morbid that way. Besides, sometimes tis just the roll of the dice. Take Boz the Draconian, I never planned on him getting killed, but he rolled a natural 1 on a savings throw, so there it is. Besides, sometimes a dying NPC stands out in the mind more than one who survives.

That being said, I do try to make a good NPC's death significant. Once again taking Boz for example, his death turned into a fairly significant plot point.

Thanks for the compliment by the way, it is nice to know that my NPCs make an impression.

Come to think of it, there have been a few NPC overlaps from my home game: Gy-Nath, Telas-Niah, Grazz the Accursed (bit character, only showed up once), Inc the Alchemist, and Zur Khan. That's it, all the others have been exclusive to Git'riban. Well, except for Firebringer Lahar, he was one of the comanders in my Incursion campaign.
