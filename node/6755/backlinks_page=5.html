Hi, Xan! This is Baloo from WOTCs chat. You told us to come check out this thread, and now I have. I tried telling you these things in the chat, but you must have had me on ignore or something, since I never saw you reply to any of these issues.

Quote:
Epic mage destruction (only requires a 4th level fighter to reach):

Blind Fight
Swarm Fighting
Mage Slayer

First, that character is illegal. You cant take Mage Slayer until 3rd level, which means you have to use your 4th level feat both for Pierce Magical Protection and Pierce Magical Concealment.

Quote:
Without magical enhancements, said fighters have a +19 to hit the wizard. No defensive casting allowed. Each spell evokes 16 AOO.
Said fighters do not have a +19 to hit. Swarmfighting maxes the bonus out at the attackers Dexterity bonus. In the chat, you said the fighters were halflings. Lets assume theyre using the elite array of stats, and put their top stat in Str (they are fighters, after all), their second stat in Dex, and increased Str at 4th level. This gives them Str 14 (15 2 race +1 level) and Dex 16 (14 +2 race). Thats a Dex bonus of +3. Combined with flanking, this gives the halflings +5 to hit the wizard. This will give them a total attack bonus of +13(+4 BAB, +2 Str, +1 size, +1 masterwork or magic; +5 situational). Also note that the Swarmfighting bonus only affects the attack roll, not damage. Second, Pierce Magical Protection doesnt undo all magical protections. It specifically only affects spells that give a bonus to AC (such as mage armor, protection from evil, shield of faith and barkskin. It does not affect spells that give DR (like stoneskin), concealment (like displacement although Pierce Magical Concealment does that, but you cant have both of them at 4th level). It wont affect the wizards bracers of armor +8, his ring of protection +5 or his possible amulet of natural armor +5 (though most wizards will prefer an amulet of health +6 over that, assuming you dont allow them to make a custom item that includes the effect of both, or that you dont let them put either effect in some other item slot). The wizard will likely have an AC of over 30, and a contingency with stoneskin in (at the very least). So the halflings will need a 17 or so to hit. That gives us three hits. Assume they use Small greatswords heck, lets make them greatswords +1. That means theyll do 1d10+4 damage (+2x1.5 Str, +1 enhancement). Most of that wont get through the wizards stoneskin an average of 1 point per attack. Even if one of them crits, thats a max of 28 points of damage, which gets reduced to 18 by the stoneskin. Since the wizard has 24 ranks in Concentration, and a Con bonus of at least +4, he has a concentration check of +28 (ignoring any items he might have that increase this). So even assuming that the 16 halflings can surround the wizard (and youd think that a 20th level wizard would have been around the block a few times and wouldnt let himself be surrounded that way), the likely outcome is: Surprise round: halflings move in and draw their weapons (if they have their weapons drawn already, the wizard will certainly not allow them to surround him). Round 1: Halflings attack (the wizard probably has a better initiative than the halflings, but lets say they rolled well). Contingency with stoneskin is activated. Wizard takes no damage, either because the halflings miss or cant penetrate DR. Wizard casts greater fireburst (from Complete Arcane, does 1d8/level (max 15d8) to all within 10 ft except the caster), provoking attacks of opportunity. One of them crits and does 20 points of damage, reduced to 10 by stoneskin. Wizard easily succeeds in Concentration check (DC 10+damage+spell level = 10+10+5 = 25) without even having to roll. Halflings take 15d8 damage, for an average of 68. Halflings have ~30 hp, so even if they save theyre dead (or dying). End result: wizard takes 15 points of damage (3 from the halflings regular attacks, 2+10 for the halflings AoOs). Halflings die, with their flesh melting from their bones. Wizard then proceeds to hunt down the halflings families as well as anyone who aided them in this endeavor, and proceeds to slaughter those as well.

To the rest of you guys, hi! Nice to meet ya!
