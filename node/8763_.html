Quote:
I have a question though: what do you use to colour your works? And what resolution do you scan the images at? The ink is so clean and crisp, it makes me wonder if I should try it out...

In coloring I mix several tehniques: I use primarly water colors and wooden pencils in color, sometimes I even use colored markers and water colors in pencils. As of black ink: I use either black marker + ink or just black marker.
But here is litle trick wich Duster showed me: when editing picture in photoshop: first he turn the picture grayscale, than it turn it back in b/w and finaly it turns it in autocontrast. (I think that is procedure but I will ask him for details).

Quote:
I love the picture of the house falling on the Hag. The book needs one or two pieces of levity.

And (dark) humor and whackynes was, is, and allways will be part of Planescape, so that was my primary inspirtion for that one: take clasic iconic moment from Wizard of Oz (Falling house on Wicked Wich of East) and turn it Planescape style.

"Whats night hags two greatest fears: falling houses and buckets of water" -Planar joke that few understand.

Arstist comment:
It took me long time but Greae (wich means gray sisters) are finaly finished. Originaly joyblood wanted hag that was driving larve with whip and eating pixie sandwich, but since Tony Diterlizzi already did something simmilar I decided to split that idea in three segmets and use them with Greae sisters from greek mythology: One sister (with eye) drives larve with staff, other sister (one with toth) eats pixie sandwich, and the third is in the middle, blind and hungry but enjoying with the rest of her sisters.

Now that I think of it, this was pretty simple picture but the most fun part (and difficult) was designing faces of hags and larve.
