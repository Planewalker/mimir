What is your favorite race?

Humans: there's something alluring about the race that gets overlooked, and something empowering about following the path to power without fancy abilities. And there's something very sweet about an extra feat and getting free choice of favored class.

Near-humans: Near-humans (such as planetouched) are also very interesting, because they allow me to make a character who functions as if he were human, but is physically different in a way that can tie into his background and personality. I play planetouched as humans with a twist--literally: planetouched are humans. They just are a different kind of human than the base homo sapiens that we know and love.

Besides that, I've always been strangely drawn to bladelings because of their mystery and their strange origins and secrets, which even they may not understand fully. And who doesn't love razorstorm? I think I first fell in love with the spiky berks when I saw the illustration in the 2e Monstrous Compendium. No other bladeling illustration has come close, in my opinion. Not porcupine quills; big spikes erupting from their skin.

And the githyanki. They're so perfect as a race of evil killers who actually work. They've managed to make a successful, (nearly) stable society based on unabashed evil that they embrace: conquest, destruction, cruelty, pride, arrogance, vengeance, and, above all, hate. The possibilities of their beliefs fascinate me. The don't try to disguise their evil, and they fully understand it. But they choose and embrace the darkness, using it as a tool to further their goals, because everything to them is an enemy to crush or a weapon to wield.
