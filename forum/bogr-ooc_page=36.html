There are actually some

There are actually some pretty good tactical uses for the levitate ability -- especially if you can levitate at will like an illithid can.

 #1:  Just levitate well out of melee range, but within convenient mind blast range.  If fighting a party of githyanki rangers, not so great, but most of the time this will significantly inconvenience an adventuring party.  Most humanoids (other than Ya'shenn) don't have a dorje of Negate Psionics around.

#2:   Levitate above a doorway with a readied grapple or Mind Blast action.

#3:  Levitate to the ceiling and move around hand-over-hand.  Especially good if the illithid has the stealth ability (rogue, assassin) or can cast or manifest invisibility.

There was a joke in the Goblins comic about an opponent with a readied action and an elevation bonus...

Levitation is indeed a useful asset, though there are some significant risks involved...

Once you go up, you make yourself a clear target for ranged attackers - no thralls to provide cover. The average githyanki band may not have plenty of archers, but they would know of an illithid's levitation ability and employ psionics that don't need to overcome PR, use thrown weapons, or possibly dimension door on top of it.

Usually, you're effectively stranded while levitating, giving the enemy time to surround and cut off escape points. Moving against a ceiling goes very slowly, and wouldn't be hard to keep up with for forces on the ground.

I imagine a flayer using #1 would see trained gith warriors jumping for cover against its mind blast, then resort to pot shots while spread out.
