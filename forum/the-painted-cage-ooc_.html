**Hear Ye and Eyeball This, Basher** Announcing a new and wonderous invention in gaming.  An adventure where the player characters { THA'S YOU } are not adventurers.  Too incredible to believe?  Nay, you will be playing artists, performers, craftsmen, and other working stiffs trying to pay the rent in that most amazing nexus of the multiverse, scenic Sigil.  Just average planars with above-average amounts of creativity which find that most incredible of instuments, that most salacious of stages, that most calamitous of canvases, the planes!

Rules (as they are, subject to change at whim)

ECL 7 ... ish.  Charactes should be artists, bards, experts, etc, spending lots of skill points in craft and performance skills.  Out-of-box thinking encouraged.  The DM {that's me} trusts all players to roll their own dice in character creation as they see fit, but the point of the game is fluff, not crunch.

Items are available in a three by three fashion.  Three major, wonderous item-type or a weapon if character concept calls for it.  Three medium power items, possibly a weapon, a secondary weapon, or something that helps AC.  Three minor magic items, "crunch" items like healing potions, minor weapons of no more than +1, whatever. 

Combat, specificly, is NOT an aim of this game.  It might happen, this is Sigil after all, but it will not take center stage.

This board will be used to brainstorm character concepts.  Post a copy of final character sheets here, so everyone can see how smart, creative, and hansome you are.

This board will also be used for general out-of-character conversation, letters to the editor, sports, gossip, love letters, horoscopes, word problems, and other various herefore unsuspected verbage.

HAVE FUN

Book of Azure's Brain.

An actual hammer and set of chisels would be cooler than a wand.  He literally scupts an illusion. 

Idea for the illusions; silent illusions only.  Three illusions going at a time, and if they do not move do not require concentration to maintain.  You can choose which illusion disipates if you want to make a new one, of course.  An illusion can be made 'real' by using two "charges" of the items.  A 'real' illusion, though still silent, is fully corporeal, and (temporarily at least) actually made of whatever material you wish to represent, in context of the sculpture.  Exotic materials, anything less mundane than rock, wood, or iron requires a third "charge".  Thus, to make something out of steel, an alloy, a precious metal or precious stone, it requires the full power of the Hammer and Chisels.  Correspondigly rarer materials have maximum sizes for the mateial able to be produced.  You could 'sculpt' an adamantite mace, or a diamond the size of a skull, but super exotic materials only a dagger or other small item.  Complex items like clocks would also require the third "charge"

Whatever your character's former affiliation, I could see the Signers, the Dustmen, and the Sensates all try to (re?) recruit him.  Perhaps some believe that eventually at some point, your spirit will merge with the Hammer and Chisels and the Faction will gain posession (pun) of a unique and powerful artifact.

All right, here are my two

All right, here are my two pitches so far. Both are elemental in nature. When one deals with the Great Wheel on a daily basis, one can often develop a fondness for the other parts of the Multiverse. I prefer more active performance like dancing, acrobatics and so on, so I am trying to figure out how to incorporate that into my two ideas below, before I settle on one.

Idea 1: A sort of glassblower, except he creates elemental sculptures. Instead of glass, he blows fire or water to create exotic, delicate forms that can be displayed just like blown glass.

Idea 2: A musician who plays "elemental strings" by plucking the flow of elements around him. Each element has a different sound and tone, and when he pulls the string, it manifests as a visible "string" of elemental power (so, a "string" of fire or water). The musician has developed this to create not only a musical performance but a fascinating visual performance of light and elemental energy.

There was a mantis-like

There was a mantis-like race in Spelljammer that secialized in organic modification, but I forget the name.  They had a write-up in a Dragon magazine a while back that was really interesting.  Basicly they could modify themselves and others, add extra limbs, etc.  The intersting thing was of course they were limited by non-magical physics, so if you, say, wanted to fly, you might wake up from the operation with your arms modified into a thirty-foot wingspan and your digestive system modified to deal with pound after pound of high-protien insect life, which by the way are your diet now.

Self-modification was a major cultural thing, not only for practical purposes but also for fashion.  Tatoos, piercings, modified limbs and carapices were the norm.

If you want to go for it, you could find out the name & stats, or use a Thri-kreen as your template and call it close enough.
