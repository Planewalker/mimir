'Dunamin' wrote:
Hmm, in that case I might consider giving my character a name change to Du-minh. I had intended it to be spoken as "Due Min".

Your choice. The differance is rather subtle, but it's there. I.e., Du'minh {Due-min} said faster vs. Du-Minh {Due Min} with more of a pause and more emphisis on the second part {min vs. Min}. It would almost be like "Minh" was his name, and "Du" was added on later.

Similarly, Gy-Nath was probably named "Nath" but his name changed as he himself advanced in his career. I see githyanki names being somewhat dynamic, like in the old oriental tradition. Thus Gy-Nath the captain, overseeing troopers with short names, and reporting to the supreme leader Ali-Gorthifsha N'khal (who may have been just "Ali" as a low-level warrior, then Ali-Khal, then Ali-Gor'khal, etc., etc.)

So, while Gy-Nath and Ali-Gorthifsha N'khal can be called Gy and Ali, respectively, a name like Jhank'kor is always "Jhank'kor", never just "Jhank", unless in battle-speak, when it might just be "Jan"
