I'll post the question now then, anybody up for DM-ing a Pratchett wise lighthearted Disk-World-like campaign, solo?:

Story connection etc,

Time Ydrass (pronounce: Tiem'maee) was a student of the Wychlaran, when she was captured by a powerful Thayan Necromancer/ Priest (Velsharoon?), who experimented on her, and created her as a greater zombie like toy, to be used at his whim. He died however at the hands of a band of Adventurers who did not loot his lab, nor killed most of his minions, meaning, a lot was left over after they were beaten back by some of his servant minions who were now free willed. Time was fortunate to have been left in a room which was locked, but not very strong, and after a few days of silence she thought to seek put if she could be free..
Whilst searching the lab for her belongings she found some items of interest and some of her original belongings, but not all she had when captured. She found a ring with 1 ruby left in it, and immediately recocnized it as a ring of 3 (1) wishes...
She used the wish with great delibiration and after some time asked for her goddesses to free all the zombies who her captor had ever created and give them a identity/ freedom of their own.
(this is how the Igorians were created)
It meant that all the zombies became intelligent and had their personalities returned, but the 2 goddesses who granted the effect also were good and decided that they would change the anatomy of the zombies in such a way that they were half living, so they would have the opportunity to live their lives slowly till they fall apart as death would still come if the body was not continued to be healed and repaired. (not finished yet, any feedback?)

FACT: I once made an Igor. Again, inspired by Mr Pratchet.

At least, the idea was similar. But I must say stat-wise Igor was much simpler.

Deep Dwarf (for a nice, sturdy base with a nicely roleplable lurch) Necropolitan (level adjustment 1 undead that is intelligent, can heal naturally and has turn resist) Artifacer (I loathe Ebberon but this seemed to fit the Igor's "I can build that" mentality) with a few levels in fleshwarper (I convinced my DM to let the class consider my artificer homunculus as my familiar, but you can spend a feat to get a familiar if your DM is more stingy. You now have 2 creatures under your command at all times.)

This character went on to create all sorts of wonderful inventions, eventually building his own extradimensional workshop.
