Re: Open Design of Dark Roads and Golden Hells

Here is some explanation of open design using the Bacon Sandwich analogy by Doomed Paladin. :

Someone approaches you and says, "I've got this great idea! It's called Bacon Sandwiches! I need help developing it though."

You say, "That sounds great, but what do you need from me exactly?"

They say, "A donation of money to purchase the materials to make them and your opinion on how they should look when we're done. A couple of other people have given me some already. Some, more than what I'm asking of you, so they get to make bigger decisions about the sandwich, like how crispy the bacon is, if there will be tomatoes, or cheese, or miracle whip, and what kind of bread we use."

You reply, "Here's what I can give you now. Can I give you more later? If I do, do I get anything other than the "privilege" of doing work for you?"

"Sure, but the longer you wait the more of the decisions will be made and the less input you'll get to put in on the finished meal. When they're all done I can sell you one 'at-cost' with what you've given me already. If you invest more you could get one plus a drink for less than you'd pay otherwise, and if you invest more than that, I'll have enough to make Bacon Sundaes for a select few."

Or, probably best, send them to the Kobold Quarterly faq: http://www.koboldquarterly.com/k/faq

Re: Open Design of Dark Roads and Golden Hells

As we near the deadline, I figure I should make a final push for people to join while the entry price is cheaper.

The project has me very excited. I find myself going through the day, thinking about new locations and new interactions for the cosmology being planned out. Here are three major things that stand out to me about this project:

1) Relationship between Good and Evil: The celestials and the fiends are the major conflict, but this a giant Cold War between the two sides. Spy games, manipulation/guidance of mortal souls, and the struggle to define the Ideals (War, Art, Freedom, etc) are major parts of this battle.

2) Planar Paths and Waystations: Portals were done masterfully by Planescape, so a major focus as been other means of travel. Sailing on the Black Ships of Leng, hitching a ride from a Valkyrie, walking the Nine Alignment Roads.

Traveling the roads one might find taverns with portals, and maybe one day your character can own them. You might also enter the Market, a place overseen by the Guardians who seem to be concerned only with stopping any conflict that might interrupt the flow of trade between the planes.

3) Relationship between Law and Chaos: One of my favorite parts, the idea that Order and Chaos can be lovers, can work together to create great Art, and meet at places such as the Great Casino which exalts the Laws of Probability (and thus Unpredictability).
