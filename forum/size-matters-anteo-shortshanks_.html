Size Matters? Anteo Shortshanks

Anteo is surrounded by light, and then he feels himself being thrust forward and sees Sophitia’s body coming up to meet him. His fall is cushioned by her though it wouldn’t really have been that bad to begin with.

She however is lying on her back in the muck of this cobbled alley that the two have landed in. When Anteo first left the light he had a split second to see Sophitia’s face, her expression terrified as she stared skyward. Then he landed with his face falling embarrassingly between he bosoms. Quickly rolling to the side, while keeping his eyes on her, Anteo is surprised to find that she did not seem to react to this, except for a cry of pain, which somehow did not disrupt her terrified upward gaze. Lying in the muck of the alley, he slowly turns his head, working his way from her face, up the wall beside her. Its architecture is strange though it appears to be the side of a building… The architecture itself doesn’t seem entirely consistent, and spikes jut from the sides like decoration. Looking higher, he reaches the top of the building, but instead of being greeted by sky or clouds, what he sees is another city above him… or below him. He isn’t certain as a feeling of vertigo momentarily overtakes him.

The terrified young

The terrified young noblewoman sits up slowly, the back of her expensive dress now smudged with large amounts of grime. Tears are in her eyes but through her terror a small hint of relief is visible as she looks down at her little lover. She opens her mouth to speak, but gets a surprised look on her face and immediately turns away to avoid evacuating her stomach contents onto him. Collapsing forwards onto her hands and knees in the muck, she coughs and wheezes for a moment, then slowly turns back to Anteo, her eyes still watering. "Anteo... W- where are... How did we get here?"

Now that he has a chance to look around, Anteo realizes that they are indeed in an alley. The ground is cobbled, rather poorly, and is covered in grime. Meanwhile the walls, with their unusual apparent mixture of alien architecture are covered with various types of graffiti. Most of it is unintelligible, though some can be made out. 'Rorinok is two hands long.' 'Garkos is three hands long' 'Perry is three and a half long.' Under these is another message. 'Selerin has 8 n ½ thanks to above.’


The only think that all the walls seems to have in common is the strange spikes jutting from all of them, and the strange vine-like things weaving their ways up the sides… The leaves of these vines seem bladelike… sharp.


Anteo and Sophitia are in a dimly lit alley. One end seems to open up into a brighter street where figures pass by every once in a while. The other end comes up in a three way intersection with a wall directly across, and another ally moving left and right. This second alley is rather darker. Aside from muck and garbage, the alley is empty. Sophitia tries to stagger to her feet and brush her hands off on the lap of her dress. She looks around forlornly and then again at Anteo is if he is the only thing in the world she can count on.

The main fire-pit is supplemented by a series of smaller pits dotted around the large entryway, providing illumination for the rest of the unlit establishment. The worst of the heat and smoke is carried out through several brass chimneys in the roof, cunningly crafted to avoid any intake of rainwater.

His gaze is immediately captured by what appears to be the illusion of a badly burned man, wreathed in flames who writhes in apparent pain as he hovers above the main pit.

The entryway is separated from the encircling taproom by a railing supported by bladed pillars as is the local style with three large, barbed arches allowing passage from one area to the other. The taproom is 3’ above the level of the entryway and grows progressively dimmer as one moves away from the flames. This area houses a scattering of sturdy tables for the mostly quiet crowd of beings who fill the bar.
