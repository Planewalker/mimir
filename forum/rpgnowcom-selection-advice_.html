Well, what you need depends on where you will be adventuring. If you're on the Nine Hells of Baator, for example, the Planes of Law and Hellbound box sets would be recommended.

Overall my favorite is the Planes of Chaos boxed set, followed by the Factol's Manifesto. The latter is great if you're playing a faction-heavy campaign in Sigil.

Planescape is all 2nd Edition Advanced Dungeons & Dragons, but it doesn't make a difference for the sourcebooks because there are so few rules in them.

The only books you would have to convert are Planewalker's Handbook and the adventures. These are stat-heavy. However, many people have already converted a lot of PS stuff to 3E so you just have to search this site to find them.

Things I suggest...

Planes of Law
Planes of Chaos
Planes of Conflict
Hellbound
Faces of Sigil

Planescape is all written in 2e. Now, this means that the stats, etc. that they give you are completely and utterly useless to a 3e player. 2e and 3e are so different its painful. Conversion back and forth is possible, but long and complex.

Luckily, PS was a stat-lite setting. Most of the books mentioned above are almost 90% flavor text and information rather than stats. Plus, alot of people have already converted much crunch for you...

This thread is currently converting almost every creature ever seen in PS to 3e: http://boards1.wizards.com/showthread.php?t=307692

EDIT: Whoops....

Oh yeah, Planewalker.com has the official conversions for races, plus new plane and faction specific feats and skills. GO THERE... DO IT....

The MotP is a good reference guide for playing in the Planes in 3.5E. It is not Planescape, however, and has a different flavour entirely. It is very much tuned to occasional forays into the planes by high-level adventurers from the prime worlds. In that way it resembles the original 1E manual of the planes.

The PlHB is mostly rules and options for players. Feats, prestige classes, monsters (?), races, and planar touchstones, which I like but many PS fans don't. It also has a decent description of Sigil, the City of Brass and Tunarath the githyanki city. Some neat equipment too.

Overall though, if you can only buy a few books, get the original PS books. They're much better. For GMs anyway.
