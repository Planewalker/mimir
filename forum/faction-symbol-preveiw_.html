Think of this as something that you would be looking at in a book, so... Things to work on:
Smoothing the edges is *definately* something I'd suggest doing.
The face on the dragon is seriously washed out by the whitelight effect that you have there. Also has a pretty serious underbite going on there.
And the back ridge of the dragon looks pretty rough, like it was just polopped down on top of the rest of the form, it's almost too stylized in comparison to the rest of it.
In the wavey bit at the top - you want to even up the curve on the bottom side to be more symmetric.

Things to keep:
Your strongest point is the gradient use - you have that down Perfectly. It really does a good job of conveying a sense of depth.
Color choice is perfect.
The overall layout of the image is strong as well - the curve of the dragon is a good form, keeps the interest.
The sword is dead on in form, and pretty much in shape and shading.
The wavey bits at the top are well done.

It's a solid rough draft really. I'd suggest possibly printing it out and taking time with the line art to get the curves going smoothly before tossing it back into the digital realm.

Ideally I don't want any of our artists to get picked on by our audience for what they put into release - hence I try to get our folks solid reviews etc in advance - b/c the wild world of fandom can get really mean. Save you and me both the hassle.
