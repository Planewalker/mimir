A little while later, two more guards appear: longtime residents of the Gatehouse might recognize these as two of the most physically strong guards in this section. They usually come out when escorting an inmate that is believed to pose a significantly greater physical threat than average. Though they seem a bit wary, the guards are neither sweating nor truly afraid, as they often are when escorting the most violent of barmies: cannibals, serial killers, arsonists, those who cut up puppies for entertainment, and so forth.

"I don't belong here," their 'guest' argues, rather calmly given the circumstances. The 'guest' in question appears to be a half-orc, with a substantial amount of something Lower Planar added in.

A very large half-orc.

A very large, female half-orc.

A very large, female, muscular half-orc.

So much so that, despite both orcish and lower-planar ancestry, she must certainly have to do a considerable amount of work to maintain that much muscle definition and tone. When not in the Gatehouse, perhaps she's a bouncer or bodyguard or some other sort of basher. Or a thug. Her voice is also deep: a contralto, and she could probably manage a countertenor without too much effort.

"Oh, we've heard that one before, haven't we?"

"Plenty of times!"

"You'd rather put me in a... a madhouse, than listen to the truth? And you call me -- me! -- the barmy one?"

"What is it this time -- Cage's portals will all open up to the Plane of Fire and burn everyone up? That was a good one."

"You forget what happened two weeks after I said that?"

"We didn't all burn up, now did we? Now, go on and meet your new... heh heh... roommates. And don't go starting any fights, either."

The guards get their new... guest... inside the room without further incident. At least this one is neither straitjacketed, attacking other inmates, nor... currently... raving. She doesn't look very friendly, though. In fact, one might receive the impression that this one firmly believes she is not insane, and is not reacting well to being shoved into a room full of 'barmies.'

One might notice further details. Her skin is a grey-green color, like that of many orcs, but those red, fiery-perdition-colored eyes practically scream out 'TIEFLING!' The very thick, black hair probably came from her orcish side. The small, surprisingly dainty-looking horns on her head did not. Despite these details, there was a human, or at least something a lot more human-appearing than an orc or a fiend, in her family tree somewhere, since her features are softer and much more human-looking than those of an orc.

She stands with her back to one of the walls, rubbing the skin around whatever bonds she's been put in (they chafe, apparently), and glowering at the other... guests... of the Gatehouse. She doesn't seem very inclined to introduce herself.

"Welcome Na'Sahlih. Welcome, Uma." The orc/tiefling quickly realizes she has not yet introduced herself by name, yet the bound medusa seems to know her already. "I am Shist, and my *ahem* companion is Indigo, both in hue and in name."

That he prefers 'the quick version' is not surprising, since though he is sitting on his bunk he rocks back and forth with barely-contained nervous energy.

"Yes, well, welcome to the reintroduction dorm. Evidently you were both judged sane enough, or safe enough, to be housed with others. The idea, I believe, is to practice interacting with other beings in preparation for an eventual release back into Sigilan society. In practice, however, I have been here for quite some time, and Indigo seems to keep coming back."

"PIKE IT, STONER! I TOLD YOU SHE TRIED TO NICK ME!" The big kobold suddenly snaps at her. He leaps off his bunk and grabs a chair. He flings it, not at her but at a random wall instead. His reptilian eyes are amber-colored, and now start to spark with tiny arcs of electricity. He whispers hoarsely in draconic and lightning dances from his claws. He grabs his own biceps and there is a loud crackling sound and the smell of ozone as he shocks himself. The action actually makes his muscles spasm and he falls to the floor. It takes a couple of minutes, but when he recovers and can stand again, Igo seems calmer, satisfied. He quiveringly climbs back on his bunk and lies down with a contented sigh.
