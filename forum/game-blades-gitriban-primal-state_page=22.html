Du'minh and Vivec search around the builing for likely hiding places for an illithid. Void Keys sits at the very top of Silver Void Street, directly opposite the huge, sprawling, Silver Void Steelworks. That is out, for it is a busy place, churning out smoke even at this post-antipeak hour. Silver Void Street terminates on Swans Way, a street far more like metropolitan Sigil than the ghetto of Git'riban. Swans Way is a wide street, perhaps twenty feet or more. Several businesses and apartments are within 100 paces of Void Keys, though it might be expected that these have been screened by the Silver Eyes. Far more likely, there is a place just under one hundred feet into Undersigil where the ghaik hide.

There are a few manhole covers up the street. Swans Way, considered "Sigil", not "Git'riban" has a sewer system in better repair than is typical in the githyanki ghetto down the hill.

One hundred paces back down Silver Void Street brings one into The Tunnels, crowded tenements built into every concievable space, above street level and below. An illithid would have a difficult time hiding here. Unless ... Unless it had a portal to the interior of one of the 'closed doors'.

You see, few blokes really pay rent in Git'riban. Instead, somebody looking for a kip will either pay a 'landlord' to find one for them, or try to stake out a place of their own. Empty apartments are usually heavily trapped (by the local 'landlords' who everybody hates because a lot of 'em are Sharpshadows), so unless one is a professional, it pays not to open a door that's closed. The custom in Git'riban is to leave a door open if expecting business or visitors. Merchants rarely put up signs (unless on the fringes of the ghetto or otherwise catering to barbarians as well as gith), and residents just know where to go and who to talk to if they need someting.

The Sewers Below Swans Way

The four gith pry open a manhole, about fifty feet down an alley off Swans Way, and decend into Sigil's underwold. They find that the drainage of Swans Way is in a state of repair that many residences in Git'riban would envy. Water flows down the center of the circular tunnels, which tend to average eight feet in diameter, though numerous pipes empty from the walls.

The center of the main passage has about six inches of flowing water. The party makes a sweep of all passages within 100 feet of Void Keys, but find nowhere an illithid could hide. None of them wants to be the first to suggest giving up, so they keep searching, expanding their sweep in case the illithids 'listening' to Mim have some way of expanding their telepathic range.

At one point Vivec notices the black rat under his shirt is lifting her nose out of his collar and sniffing the air intently. The party passes a pipe of about four foot diameter high on the wall which spills only the barest trickle, but as they pass it by, Ista K'ait tweaks Vivec painfully on the chest.

Barely half a moment before this, Draz's attention is brought to the pipe because the trickle of water coming from it sounds a little different than others like it. He notices cracks in the porcelin around the pipe which the watter splashes through.

The Temple of Tiamat

With a single strong stroke, Ar'dru lops off Lind's arm. Though she tries very hard to be brave, Lind screams when her arm is severed and falls to the floor. Ar'dru examines the arm and notes with pride that the spreading mark had not reached his cut, which fell about halfway between elbow and shoulder. As he watches, the poison takes over the arm, turning the golden scales and flesh black. A black stain even spreads part-way out into the pool of blood gushing from the severed arm.

"Hrrg, Nyaarg, b ... bastards! The Avernians took my priests, didn't they?" Lind stands and casts an incantation in draconic, summoning a ball of flame into her hand, and burns the stump. She snarls and stumbles over to the altar. She frees the terrified gnome, who is too frightened and shocked to even run. Lind then picks up the severed arm and places it on the altar. She does not thank Ar'dru, but gives him a polite bow. She tells tells both him and the gnome that they may stay or go as they desire, then sets about righting the tumbled ceremonial furnishings before kneeling before the altar.
