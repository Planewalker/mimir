Hmmm both ideas are very intriguing. I'll let everyone in on a few details I can divulge from Azthri's biography without giving everything away, keeping in mind that I am more than willing to alter just about anything.

1) Azthri's mother was a member of the Chrysocolla Band who suffered a terrible accident while on a raid against the githzerai in Limbo when carrying Azthri. The trauma was severe enough to break her mind and she fled to Sigil under the delusion that her warband was compromised. This trauma affected the unborn Azthri as well, but in a more insidious manner than most will expect. O_o

2) The egg containing Azthri was laid in the basement of a Hive soup kitchen, where the now completely insane warrior hid from the 'ghaik thralls' she continually ranted about to the soup kitchen overseer. The overseer took the newborn the day she hatched while Azthri's mother had fallen into one of her stupors and turned her over to a local orphanage.

3) Azthri ran away from the orphanage at the githyanki equivalent of thirteen, worked as a light-boy for one year, then fell into working as a Collector for five years under the 'protection' of a cross-trading couple who sent their 'apprentices' out to fetch as many corpses for the Dustmen as possible.

4) Managed to find work in the Market Ward as an 'errand-berk' for a somewhat shady doctor who exploited Azthri's githyanki heritage as a means to scare off creditors. Earned her nickname 'Plague-Healer' due to her astute quick-thinking that averted a potential epidemic when her employer unwittingly contracted bubonic plague. In one of his rare moments of gratitude, the doctor made his errand-berks assistants. Seven years later all of them, including Azthri, were full-fledged doctors themselves.

5) So where does the 'militant' part of the 'militant rogue' come into play? Azthri's occupation enabled her to work in Git'riban with little trouble other than the occasional snide remark. Naturally, many of her patients were outcast githyanki from the old regime and reveled in her enthusiastic interest in their former lives in Tu'narath. One such warrior felt it was an absolute waste for such a smart young githyanki to stoop to 'ghustil's work' and personally trained Azthri for a decade using the same grueling techniques she herself had been subjected to as part of her training decades before in her own warband.

6) Azthri has gone to Starmantle at the urging of her warrior mentor to 'find a better class of the People than these ghetto-bred rats and make something of yourself!' Has somewhat of a tendency to idolize/hero-worship those githyanki she finds worthy of emulating. Seems like our group of Git'ribani is going to have a fan......XD

Additional facts: She is true neutral and still unsure of what faction she believes in despite having some disturbingly Reconcilliationist leanings, making her ripe for any member of the Git'ribani to 'convert' her to their own philosophies. Evil!Chaotic_Goth1431 says Evil!DM and other players should use this to an advantage!!!!

The 'insidious' repercussions of the accident in Limbo will be revealed here should Azure or Zimmy request it. I admit it will be a trifle....odd, but will give the character a significant weakness to balance her out. =D
