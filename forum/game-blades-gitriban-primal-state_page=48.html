Now inside the skiffs, the

Now inside the skiffs, the githyanki have a higher view of the situation.  Looking or shooting out the arrow slits is somewhat dangerous at first, but less so once the skiffs get underway.  The githyanki see that the palace is in a walled compound with several buldings.  The entire compound sits atop a prominant rise within the city around, but it is not the only one.  Similar palace compounds dot the hills of Hlondeth, which is a very large city.  The githyanki here knew exactly which yuan-ti nobles were harboring the illithid.

The sun is just beginning to break on the horizon.  It is less than an hour after the githyanki assault began, and it can be seen that the state of alarm has just reached the city itself.  Two dozen githyanki skiffs still circle the compound, firing arrows, their alchemical grenades and pots already spent.  The gitribani are lucky, there is barely enough room in those skiffs that carried pots instead of warriors for the increased number of githyanki.  That, and Kirath is informed that there were five casualties, though the bodies were recovered.

The skiffs widen their circles, and begin to move off toward the open sea to the southeast.  Just as they do, six larger ships dip below the clouds.  They begin dropping fire pots on the yuan-ti compound.  Lots of them.  These flame pots do not eplode with large bangs, but rather shatter where they hit, spaying the area with a dark red liquid that quickly starts burning as soon as it contacts air.  The flames do not produce much smoke, and the liquid is runny.  It does its job.  Less than a minute after the first pass, every building in the compound is on fire.  The astral brigs come in for a second pass, hitting those few areas less affected, and spreading the fire to the surrounding city as well.  Meanwhile, the skiffs fly over the center of the city, near a prominant temple-pyramid ...

[At this point Kirath has to make a decision, will he issue the order to drop all the illithid and yuan-ti heads taken?  It would be best to leave no doubt as to why Hlondeth was attacked, but the order will not be popular, and especially unpopular with the PCs who have taken heads here as well as in Sigil.  The PCs then have to decide if they are going to chuck the other heads they've taken.  The more illithid heads hit the streets of Hlondeth, the stronger the message, and technicly since Gy-Nath symbolicly gave ultimate command to Kirath, the order would actually include all illithid heads.  Basicly, I'm setting up a twist of inter-party conflict, but I don't want to railroad you.  Kirath can issue the order ... or not.  The PCs can obey ... or not.]

A rain of heads come from

A rain of heads come from the skiffs.  They are about 25% illithid, the rest being yuan-ti nobles.  They mostly bounce down the sides of the temple-pyramid.  Bells ring throughout the city.  Kirath, his order given, fades into unconsiousness as one of his warriors transports him to one of the brigs for treatment of his poisoned wound.  Just before he goes out, he recieves a report; bells are ringing throughout the city, and the compounds of the other noble houses are coming to life.

The dawn is a red one in Hlondeth.  A great rising stain of smoke comes from the burning palace.  The githyanki pilots form up after droping the heads.  They head west, over the port and towards the sea, instead of the way they came.  The yuan-ti of Hlondeth, now sturred up like a nest of snakes poked repeatedly, show that they are defenceless.  Great spears, fired by balistas, shoot up from the fortifications by the port.  Several hit, with enough force to rend the steel armor of the skiffs, though the ones that hit the brigs break.

A a balista bolt the size of a lance penetrates the skiff in which Ya'shenn and K'tch ride.  In an incredible stroke of luck, it pierces the side of the pilot's head, killing him instantly.  Ya'shenn must quickly push his lifeless body aside, move the spear enough to take his place, and take control of the skiff.  As she grasps the control skull she is suddenly far more aware of the surroundings.  She can look in any direction as if she were the skiff itself, and her cairvoyant powers, already spent from the battles in Illithid House, are recharged.  She sees that the yuan-ti do indeed have their own air cavalry.  A dozen war wyverns with riders rise from one of the noble compounds.  They are soon joined by two dozen more from other compounds.  Though the skiffs can outrun them easily, the wyverns are slowly gaining on the brigs.  With the brigs is also the skiff damaged from ramming the stone gazeebo, and it makes a very inviting target.

More volleys come at the githyanki, but they are soon out of range, over the Vilhoon Reach.  About fifty miles wide at this point, the other side is not visible.  Ya'shenn is contacted by Kirath's coordinator [will edit name].  She informs the fleet that Kirath has been poisoned and is unresponsive.  She herself assumes command of the fleet and orders the skiffs to defend the brigs and the damaged skiffs.  The brigs form a three-point defensive pocket around the damaged skiffs (the gazeebo-crasher and two more that took multiple ballista hits).  The maneuvers slow the fleet down slightly, and the wyvern-riding yuan-ti, bent on revenge, urge their mounts to catch up quickly.
