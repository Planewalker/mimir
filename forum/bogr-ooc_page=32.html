1 AoO per round, maximum. That is, unless you have combat reflexes feat, in which case 1 AoO per opponent, to a maximum of AoOs = dex mod per round. So still only 1 AoO in this case anyway.

Illithic head-grab works like thus (as per 3.0 rules, which I have) : Tenticles attack, the ones that hit are attached. The illithid can try to attach the rest with an opposed grapple next round (which does not itself provoke an AoO), if successful all 4 are attached. On its turn on the 3rd round, IF all 4 tenticles are still attached, the illithid rips out Ii's brain.

Quote:
i have dam red. 5/+1 so very little to no damage actually, can he even bite through my skin? :twisted: i mean +4 natural ac and dam redux is not making that easy

Well, since a tenticle does d4+1, Ii takes no actual damage. That is, the damage taken does not slow Ii down, not that the illithid can't get through her skin. I'd understand if you argued with me, but I think its a little extreme to say Ii can't be de-brained. Its just that she's so tough that the tenticle damage has no real effect on her combat-wise.

DR as listed has no effect on grapples. For that matter, all I'd have to do is rule Lavendar has a feat to increase tenticle damage, or apply a strenth mod, and Ii would possibly take some damage, which means anything accompanying the damage happens as well.

So: DM ruling is that Ii takes no HP damage from the tenticle attacks, but still has to worry about that 4 tenticle grapple.
