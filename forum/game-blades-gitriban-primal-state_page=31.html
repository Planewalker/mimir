Git'riban, built on the ruins of the older elvin district, has many hidden ways, tunnels, alleys, vacant lots, and ruins. It would be difficult for anyone to take a route through the district that is open to the Sigilan 'sky' throughout, unless they stuck to Gith Lane or Silver Void Street.

Thus, when the githyanki slip out of Git'riban, they do so in ways harder to follow than a Slaad's train of thought.

****

The Square of the Mind is a large public space filled with flat rounded cobbles, worn smooth by water. Three great angular rocks, ranging from twelve to twenty feet tall, stand like islands. The entire place is a titanic zen garden, donated by Zadara to the elven nobles after the Forest burned, before Git'riban.

In the shadow of the tallest rock, Daraz waits. Ya'shenn's boarding house is nearby, and she had gone to fetch what jink she could. The rain has quickened, now falling in large, warm drops. As Ya'shenn approaches, Daraz shakes his head, for he can hear her jinx clinking in the purse she grabbed. He takes off his cloak, and lets himself be soaked by the rain. He shows her how to roll the coins in the cloth cape before stuffing the whole affair into the now-straining shoulder-bag. "This does three things," he explains, "It is simply quieter, it does not advertise wealth, and in a pinch, if you pull the whole cloak out, the coins go everywhere, causing a distraction."

"You're getting soaked ..." Ya'shenn shows concern, then silently chides herself for automaticly trusting Daraz without thinking.

"Better to wash the smell of Git'riban off anyway."

Ya'shenn and Daraz walk through the Clerks' Ward near the Festhall District, both without raincloaks.

****

The Sandstone District was once one of the most prosperous districts in the Clerks' Ward, dispite the fact that it bordered on the Hive Ward. The district is composed of large, sturdy buildings of sandstone. It had a well-made wall, thirty-three foot high, separating it from the slums. No wall, however, could stop the relentless march of poverty. The Sandstone District, so close to the Hive, was targeted by desperate criminals for its wealth and location. The residents fortified their homes further, or moved to other neighborhoods. The vacant buildings filled with squatters, and eventually the economy tanked. Now the Sandstone District is held up as an example by those who say the Hive Ward is growing. The buildings and streets still have the strong, simple elegance of the Clerks' Ward, but it is considered the Hive Ward by all Sigilans. At the near edge is Sandstone Square. Here the two Wards mesh. While the Sandstone District is considered "one of the best neighborhoods in the Hive", Sandstone Square is more like "the worst neighborhood in the Clerks' Ward". Here, businesses and professionals hold out in the face of anarchy. Of course, most of those businesses and professionals that have remained (or moved in) cater to Hivers and Under-Sigilans with jink to spend. There is a Key Shop, two Shiv-Shops, an Armorer, and an Alchemist, all very large affairs occupying several huge, sandstone buildings.

The first to arrive are Du'minh and Ar'dru. They do not even get to the door of the alchemists' shop, however, before Gy-Nath and Ii'Jyka'Vaar arrive in Sandstone Square, with Ya'shenn and Daraz close behind. The six gith enter the shop.

Inside, the front room has a conter on which are empty flasks demonstrating fluid measure sizes. There does not seem to be anybody minding the store. In the back, behind the counter, is a low hubub of noise, which gets louder as the githyani investigate. They walk into the back to find a behive of activity. The interior is a large space with many tables and partitions. Alchemists of several different races are working, precicely measuring out ingredients, cleaning glassware, calibrating scales, etc. The most common by far, however, are the legions of kobolds carrying things back and forth. Nobody even notices the gith.

Gy-Nath stops one of the kobolds by standin in its way, "Inc." he says.

The kobold points with its snout, and Gy wlaks back to find an ebony-scaled kobold, much larger than the rest. The black kobold is arguing with an aged human about the temperature of kiln number three.

"When I say five hundred seventy-three degrees, I don't mean 'oh, somewhere between five seventy and five seventy-five' you sodding berk! Now get back therer and make sure its at five hundred seventy-three. Five hundred seventy-three! Five hundred seventy-three! Understand? If that batch goes in and the kiln is at five seventy-two, I'll throw you in with it!"

He glances over towards the approaching githyanki, and at first seems to ignore them a moment before doing a double-take.

"Oh, it's you ... " Inc says when he recognizes Gy-Nath. "Sorry cutter, but I've got a really big order and a tight scedule, so whatever it is, it'll have to wait a few days. That is, unless you want incendiaries, I could sell you a few out of this order if you're prepaired to pay a little more than my client per unit," Inc winks.

As Gy-Nath talks to the half-dragon Inc, Ar'dru notices that some of the kobolds, though they ignored the party's entrance and are still hard at work, steal some glaces at the duthka'gith's back.

Ar'dru bids the rest good hunding and takes to Sigil's skies. The party sees him circle back a few times, watching for anyone following. Ya'shenn and Daraz go forward towards the portal the Accursed showed them, a back-alley affair just outside the Festhall District whose portal key is 'a copper coin clenched in the mouth'. The three armored warriors take a longer route to the same place.

The trio of githyanki warriors find themselves marching almost in step, for here in the crossroads of the planes even the most jaded bashers hold the githyanki in awe and fear, and for their own part, githwarriors often seek to encourage this kind of thinking in the graith. Gy-Nath talks easily with Du'minh and Ii'jyka'vaar in their native tounge. He asks nothing about their pasts nor their political affiliations, but they get the sense that the captain holds himself as a Unifist, one who believes in the githyanki people's ability to transcend the passing of Vlaakith and emerge more powerful for the shake-up. "The Great War is all, my brother and sister. Though our siblings may bicker, they know this too. Let our example inspire them. I have allied with many graith who have seen the truth of The Enemy, and will continue do so whenever it slays ghaik. Look at them watch us, the mixed stew of a million planes, realms, and worlds. See how those that know us fear us, and how those who are clueless wonder if they should. Let them, too, know of our example, and let those that can comprehend honor and courage see us as exemplars of these things. We stand before the doom of the multiverse and quail not!"

There is also a male tiefling, a well-dressed and slyly handsome chap in a top-hat with much gold jewelry, who is smoking a large cigar. He seems armed only with a fancy cane, but Daraz has seen the sort. Likely, a twist of the head and he'd draw a shiv from it, or a blade'll pop out the end, or something similar.
