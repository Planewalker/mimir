As a DM I've long done what I call a "critical strange" rule. Critical hits (and misses) should not just have mechanical advantage (or ...) they should also affect the plot. Thus, I often make a natual 1, or sometimes when THE number needed to succeed is rolled, it is a "critical strange". Maybe it works, but not in the way the PC intended, or something backfires, or it opens an opportunity for someone else, or something else happens that has lasting effects on the campaig, or the EvilDM comes out of the closet.

Keeps the DM on his toes creatively.
