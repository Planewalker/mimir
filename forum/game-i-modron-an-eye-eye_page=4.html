The modrons all filed out of the shop but not before A'kin managed to give each of them pieces of red and green candy that smelled of delicious fruits and were made in the shape of famous Sigilians. The slaad and his cab were still waiting outside. "Get what ya' needed, Master S.I.D.R.E.M.?" he inquired, helping the spiked modron onto the top of the cab. The rogues quickly and efficiently piled into the taxi wagon and with a snap and a guffaw and a noise that sounded something like a bird in a pasta-maker the slaad-controlled vehicle dissappeared into the Sigilian streets.

A'kin watched the modrons leave from the window. As they raced into the night, the arcanaloth smiled and pulled the curtain shut. He moved about the shop silently as a shadow, locking up cases and breaking down displays. Turning to the doorway he drew forth a plain key, quickly locked the single unassuming doorlock, and turned the sign on the door to "Out".

With all of this done, A'kin turned and strode purposefully towards the back of the store, a thin trail of ash following his every step. The backroom of the store was a small closet where the fiend kept some of the more strange and exotic wares, it was also where he kept some brooms and other implements and where he stashed a bottle of Krynnish Dwarven Spirits for when he was feeling exceptionally drained. This was not the room to which he retired.

Oh sure, it was the doorway to that room that he stepped through, and most anybody will say that if you walk through the same doorway twice you'll get to the same place both times. But either A'kin had not heard this saying or he simply did not care, for the room he entered was not the one that the multiverse expected him to step into.

The room was a rather large chamber made of stone. On the floor was traced a hexagram circumscribed in a triangle and candles were arranged around it. Moving across the shadowed chamber, the yugoloth strode to the center of the glyph on the floor and seated himself in a cross-legged position. The yugoloth closed his eyes and let his mind wander afar.

"They have come and gone," he said, at last in a sonorous tone that seemed to hit notes in octaves at right angles to the normal musical scale.

"Excellent, do they suspect duplicity?" another voice could be heard, but its source could not be seen by any eyes but A'kin's.

"It is obvious that they do not trust Eye and one seemed quite shaken by myself, but no, I do not think that they suspect anything specific as of yet."

"Excellent, excellent..."

The conversation stretched further and hours later the Friendly Fiend stood to his feet, shaking off a thin layer of ash that had formed on his fur and walked back into the shop, a jaunty tune whistling from between his canine lips.

The Hands of Time. Even in the darkness of the Sigilian night, the building was a sight to behold. The flickering streetlights of the Lower Ward glittered off of rotating gears and flywheels and pistons, the entire building was a vast device the likes of which the gnomes of Krynn would sell half of Mount Nevermind to study. The travelers were struck by how quiet the neighborhood surrounding the building was. Sure, the building itself was making more noise than a dancing tyrannosaurus, but the streets, they were silent. Located deep in the neighborhood known to Sigilians as Gear Run, the Hands of Time was usually bustling with activity at all hours of the night it was the central meeting place for the rogue modrons of Sigil and served as almost a temple to the home plane of Mechanus. But on this night there were no modrons about. A few mortals were standing about most of them showing obviously lawfully influenced genetics, but the vast majority of Gear Run inhabitants were the modrons and they were conspicuously absent.

Yarrdulence stopped the vehicle and hopped down off of it. He helped SIDREM to the ground, after politely taking back his hat (which he had lent to the rogue rogue modron for the ride), and then opened the carriage door to allow the other modrons out. Quiet as a foundry round here, Yarrdulence muttered, Where did all of the other boxes go?

Doubtless the assumption is due to Optimal's non-traditional physical makeup. This is due to a side-effect of Optimal's psychometabolic experiments while on the plane of Limbo. The poor lonely modron was destined to not recieve an answer to its questions, not at that point anways. For as its words hung in the air, a loud crash of lightning resounded outside. Rain began pounding the glass ceiling of the Hands of Time, drops of water found the cracks in the stonework and sprinkled down onto the modrons before, with a loud whirr, the rain shield extended across the roof sealing any joints or holes which were designed to keep the delicate machinery well cooled.

The, now wet, modrons were struck by the oily texture of the rain water and the acrid coppery smell and taste of it. Between the flashes of lightning (which were coming one after the other after the other) they could see that the rainwater was a deep crimson color, the color of fresh blood. Perhaps it was time to end their errands and scurry back to their homes to wait out the terrifying and unseasonable weather.
