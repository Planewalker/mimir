The uppermost floor of the

The uppermost floor of the offices is smaller than the rest, with a single window offering a view of the entire Packing House. Evidently, this is how Bliss obseved all the action from the beginning. There are no catwalks up this high, and Ar'dru must use both hands to carry Du'minh. Therefore he cannot do so and ready a genade. (Assuming the duthka'gith sets the Rish-i-chal down on the reminants of the upper catwalk. Du'minh meets the rest of the party coming up the stairs as Ar'dru, levitating, opens up his pack. [Ar'dru can ready any of the remaining incendiaries he wishes, just in case it IS a trap, to chuck into the shattered window.] )

Daraz hangs back as a rear-guard and support at Gy-Nath's orders.

The rest of the githyanki climb the last flight of stairs to find the door ajar. They rush in ...

Bliss could not flee. Unlike the others, who wore punishment collars but wandered the Packing House otherwise freely, Bliss's collar is chained to a desk. There are several abacusses and a book-case full of large ledgers in the small office. Bliss itself sits in an uncomfortable-looking chair behind the desk, and just behind Bliss stands a horror straight from a nightmare.

The second illithid glares at the entering githyanki with its one good eye. The other is just an empty socket, and indeed the entire left side of its face is horribly mutilated by acid burns. The two tenticles on that side are little more than stumps. In its right hand is a meat cleaver, while in its left is a large spoon, both slick with blood.

At the githyankis' entrance, Bliss looks at them pleadingly, but it is too late. With a deft sweep of the cleaver, Pig-Killer chops at the crown of Bliss's skull. The pacifistic illithid screeches in pain, pain which does not subside quickly, for Pig-Killer's swipes do not kill its victim. The maimed illithid casts a hateful glare at its mortal enemies and sends telepathicly with such strenth that it makes their teeth ache.

~ Do you enjoy irony? We have plenty of it in here! I was named the Pig-Killer for I was this place's original inmate. It was aquired for my torture, to consume only dull-witted livestock. Now, after years of confinement in the collar, I find myself finally and joyously free! Yet so maimed am I that I cannot do that which I most desire, to tear out a brain and consume its succulent sentience. This one, my opposite, who refused to consume unwilling sentients, will now experience my greatest joy and its own greatest antithema: the taste of illithid brain, its own!

Pig-killer dives in greedily with the bloody spoon. Bliss's screams pierce the air, and that is not all. Pig-Killer telempathicly projects its insane, sadistic rapture outward.

[DC 17 will save, those that fail are frozen in horror as they try to close off their minds. Those that succeed can shut Pig-Killer out and act. Ar'dru, if he's levitating outside the window, does not need to save]

"Now, siblings, for our next phase.  From here, we head down into the drains.   There we will find a portal to Mudshow's lair.  We are unlikely to encounter any illithid there other than Mudshow itself, as I got the impression that the illithid dislike using the area dispite its wealth of portals.  Once we deal with the Mudshow itself, we will find a portal that leads to the sewers under the Prime Palace.  Once there, we will meet up with K'tch's group.  Being on a prime world instead of in Sigil, we need no longer fear forming a strikeforce large enough to attract The Lady's attentions.  This is good, because we should hit the Palace hard.  Very hard.  We have done well in disrupting Illithid House's operations in the Hive Brothel and here in the Packing House, but these were high-profit yet smaller scale operations.  The Palace, on the other hand, brings an entire Prime nation under the ghaiks' control through their entrallment of the imperial family.  Ideally, we should not leave a single one alive.  I know not how many ghaik we may encounter there, we will certainly slay any we do, but our primary mission will be to disrupt their control.  In our favor, we will not likely meet considerable resistance, as the guards surround the Palace but few are allowed within to mingle with the ruling family.  Still, our strike should be as swift as completeness allows, as there is a possibility at least that an entire army of pawns stands ready to protect their rulers."
