[OOC: Assuming here that Daraz appears to be stabilized enough for Ya'shenn to leave him alone for a few seconds.]

Less familiar with the atavistic rage than some of her kind, it takes the psion longer to recognize that the ghaik is truly dead, and longer for the searing hatred to start to wind down. It doesn't help that the psion is genuinely afraid of Tamers, above and beyond other illithids. Mind flayers despise their rebellious ex-thralls as a matter of course -- but killing Gith's children is part of a Tamer's mission in life, and they tend to be very good at it.

She's very aware of their prowess in physical combat, and it seems almost impossible that she, with her limited expertise when it comes to physical combat, had just finished off one of them with a blade.

Had we not just been incredibly lucky, that Tamer would be wearing our skulls as trophies.

~Daraz...?

'Azure' wrote:
The Bloodstone walks up behind Ya'shenn and puts a hand on her shoulder.

He is certainly taking this calmly! For a few moments Ya'shenn envies him that trait she's observed in so many of her brothers, their insensitivity to violence. At least now she knows, spattered by the blood of an illithid warrior, that at least she's not a complete coward. In some things.

Quote:
He then bows to Ya'shenn, "I am Lahar the Burning Mind, Firebringer of the Band of Bloodstone."

Firebringer...?! Did he really just say--?!

It's only through considerable effort of mind, and exercise of will, that the Double-Edged forces herself to stand. The effort of killing the Tamer has put her badly off balance. The part of the brain -- not often put to use, in her case -- that guides a sword blade shares little in common with the centers that control etiquette and polite speech, and for a few moments Ya'shenn has genuine difficulty accessing the latter.

The psion bows as deeply and formally as Lahar's vaunted title demands. "You honor us with your presence, Firebringer." Ya'shenn is about to say something else, then looks the Bloodstone directly in the eyes as something -- ever so belatedly -- occurs to her. The Tamer (Tamers?) had fully occupied her attention until this moment.

It isn't common, but there have been red-haired githyanki since the Liberator's generation -- probably even longer than that. The eyes, though, are very abnormal. And there was the way his mind had felt, and the fact he's a pyrokineticist...

He is a duthka'gith.

While Ya'shenn is skilled at concealing her emotions, she's not certain that she can completely obscure the instinctive flash of distaste she feels from a psion of Lahar's power. Then there is that brief startled look, one that the half-dragon has probably seen before, when a githyanki realizes his true race.

"It's fortunate indeed," she continues, recovering, "that you were in that particular place just then. At the time, I had good reason to believe one of these ghaik would be heading for the surface. It seems they changed their minds. I am grateful for your aid, and even moreso for the loan of your blade. This fight would have been much more -- unpleasant -- otherwise." The psion means what she says... mostly... even if she doesn't feel all that grateful just now.
