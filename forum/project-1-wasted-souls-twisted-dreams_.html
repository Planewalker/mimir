Some more info on how to contribute:

Chapter 1: I’ll write this up when all other chapters are ready

Chapters 2 & 5: These are meant to be something like the introductions in „Faces of Evil: The Fiends“. The only difference is, we don’t need to stick to one text, and the lengths may vary. We should stay at three or four entries per chapter, though. I myself will definitely write one per chapter.

Chapters 3 & 6: Every entry in this chapter should contain the following:
- Name, Location, Classes (if available)
- Character background including personal aims
- The Dark: Most important secrets of this character
- Statistics (optional): For Night Hags only if they differ from the standard Night Hag entry; for Hordlings a basic stats block containing ability scores and special abilities, immunities and vulnerabilities

Chapter 4 & 7: Adventure outlines should be at least one page long, but no more than three pages. Adventure seeds can be anywhere between a paragraph and half a page. These adventures are not yet meant to spill any major darks of Night Hags & Hordlings, they are meant to introduce players and DMs alike to the feeling of playing adventures in the Gray Waste, and of how to deal with our two featured races (and what to expect from them).
I myself will write up at least one adventure outline for each chapter. The Hordling adventure outline will be about facing a „real“ Horde, i.e. a ravaging mass of Hordlings out to destroy anything in their way... but, of course, with a twist.

Chapter 8: I like how Armoury99 wrote up locations in the following thread: /forum
We should stick to that format for future locations.

Chapter 9: Spells and magic items are written up in the standard 3.5 format. Please focus on evil magic; spells and items to counter the Horrors of the Waste will be published in future PDFs.
Rituals tend to be more vague than spells, while on the same time being more powerful. Descriptions shouldn’t be in stats-blocks like we know it from spells, rather they should include a flavour description ending with the following:
- Short summary of the ritual’s effect (one or two sentences)
- Necessary parts of the ritual (short list, like „Mistletoe and the finger of a god“)
- Premises and backfire effects: What needs to be done to find out about the rite, and what can happen merely by knowing the ritual
- Characters (optional): Individual characters that know about the ritual, or that can help in finding out or performing it

Chapter 10: Skills and feats of the nasty kind, which could be learned by Night Hags and Hordlings, or teached to characters that serve them or are otherwise linked to them.

Giddy feeling of excitement, most unWaste-like! This all looks great. Notes and comments:

The location format is taken from the 2e Planescape Box Set, if anyone want's a peak for further info. I'll summarise the guidelines here though (there's a similar-looking guide to Realms as well):

Quote:
Character. Just because it's not living, that doesn't mean a town's got no personality. Character's part the general alignment of the place, and part the nature of what happens there.

Ruler. This tells a sod who's officially in charge - name, race, class, and level - and what that supposed chief is like to deal with. Be sure to check the next entry to find out who's really in charge.

Behind the Throne. Just because a berk rules a town, it doesn't mean he's the only boss. Most times there's one or more other groups trying to pull the strings. A wise cutter takes time to find out who's really the high-up man in the local operation.

Description. Here, the layout of the town's presented so that things said later'll make sense.

Militia. Player characters seem to get messed up with the local authorities a lot. This entry tells who's in charge, what they've got to command, and just what kind of attitude they're likely to have in response to the mischief player characters are likely to cause.

Services. This is the same as the entry for realms: a listing of places to go and things to buy that could be useful to a band of danger-loving toughs.

Local News. Gossip's always useful, and a wise cutter keeps his ear to the ground. This entry gives some of the chant the locals are likely to know.

I'll definitely do at least one adventure outline for one section or another - probably expanding on one of the hooks in my previous posts.
