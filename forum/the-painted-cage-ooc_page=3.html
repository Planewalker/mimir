Here's a (very) rough

Here's a (very) rough draft. Your comments are welcome.

General concept: Knife-thrower/juggler/acrobat, with some skill with musical instruments thrown in. 'Exotic weapon feats' also applies to 'William Tell' displays with crossbows, etc.

Illonis Bard 4/Fighter 2

STR 12, DEX 21, CON 12, INT 16, WIS 12, CHR 16

Feats: Point-Blank Shot, Precise Shot, Quick Draw, Far Shot, Weapon Focus (daggers)

Skill ranks: Balance 7, Climb 4, Diplomacy 5, Jump 4, Knowledge (the planes) 5, Listen 7, Perform (exotic weapon feats) 7, Perform (string instruments) 7, Sleight of Hand 7, Swim 2, Tumble 7, Speak Language (Auran), Speak Language (Draconic), Speak Language (Orcish), Speak Language (Dwarven) (free), Spot 2
Skill bonuses: Balance +12, Climb +5, Diplomacy +8, Jump +5, Knowledge (the planes) +8, Listen +9, Perform (exotic weapon feats) +12, Perform (string instruments) +12, Sleight of Hand +12, Spot +3, Swim +3, Tumble +12

Languages: Common, Dwarven, Elven, Celestial, Infernal, Auran, Draconic, Orcish

Illonis racial abilities/modifiers: Ambidexterity feat, +2 Dex, +2 Cha, -2 Str, +2 racial bonus to Perform, one free language, favored class of Bard, Blind (possessing blindsight), ECL +1

Signature magical item: 'Twinblades of Arven the Unscrupulous.'

Many years ago, a half-elven Xaositect mage called himself "Arven the Inscrutable," but he is better known by later generations as "Arven the Unscrupulous." An unreliable manufacturer and seller of magical weapons, he crafted the Twinblades in order to wreak a terrible vengeance on a rogue who had swindled him. Their first property is simple enough: through minor illusionary magic, they can appear to have a fiery aura, be faintly luminscent, exude a dark menacing mist, feel warm or cool in the wielder's hand, and so on. They can also take on the style and appearance of various crafted daggers (i.e., a typical and boring blade, a beautiful masterwork blade, a mithril blade, a gith-forged dagger, and so on.) Second, they act as +4 weapons when used for display purposes only. If the wielder attempts to use them in actual combat, their 'bonus' drops to -4. The wielder is free to use another weapon in battle if she chooses, but the blades are (marginally) intelligent, and will literally whine incessantly if the wielder uses another weapon in real combat. (Not a good situation for a stealthy rogue.) They can be quite jealous of other weapons, especially highly magical ones. They also have an unfortunate tendency to return to the wielder's possession, even if given away, sold, stolen, or thrown into the nearest portal. During the course of, say, bardic performances, they're usually well-behaved, even if they're not the center of attention. The Twinblades may be useless to rogues, but they have been passed around the bardic community for many years now.
