Secondarily. I was considering something. How do we want to run rogue modrons?

Modrons of any caste can go rogue, and modrons have the documented ability to command modrons of a lower caste than they - thus making rogue heirarchs espcially dangerous. Yet the default rogue modron player race is a quadrone variant (wings but no flight).

I was thinking that the reason for this is that, upon first going rogue modrons retain their shape and status amongst other modrons, but only while on Mechanus. Once the modron leaves Mechanus its tie to the Energy Pool is completely cut and, after a period of weeks, the modron's body degenerates into a more base form. (This is going with the assumption that Mono, Duo, and Tridrones are all too basic to go rogue - well, I suppose there could be rogue Tris) Thus, the majority of rogues in the planes are quadrone shaped (another reason is that there are more quadrones than any of the heirarchs).

These are just my thoughts on the matter.

Yeah, I've been thinking about this character concept for a while; I even plan on using him in my novel trilogy when I get to it this summer. To tell you the truth, the idea for him sorta came from Nordom. The fact that Nordom was able to change his wings into arms, always got me thinking that it would be cool if Modrons could modify their bodies in other ways, and the egoist seemed like the best way to do it. I also find it funny to call a being that formerly had no sense of self an "Egoist."

Oh, as for his appearance, I've decided that it changes. His surface metalic parts can change to diferernt types of metals, stone, glass, bone, hardwood, plastic, chiton, ceramic, etc.; his skin changes colors, can change to scales, gain feathers, grow hair, be silky soft, or rough as sandpaper, or any other type of flesh. This of course would have absolutely no stat effect, and would only change while Optimal rests to regain Power Points; afterward it would stay as is until he rests again.

Oh, and while not researching/practicing in Limbo, Optimal works for a crazy da Vinci-esque engineer. He helps organize the place, and acts as a sounding board for the inventor for pay, and instruction in engineering (so as to better understand his own mechanical side).
