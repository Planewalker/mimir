Trogs are an interesting race, I had fun exploring them when I ran Return to the Temple of Elemental Evil. There's this rogue-cleric in the campaign whom I turned into a semi-recurring villian, and it struck me - how can trogs be stealthy with that stench? Both are actually NPCs in my other PS game, though N'Gar Brother-Eater started off as an NPC in another campaign I ran too! I had an old Dragon Mag with "The Ecology of the Flind" I used to base him on. He was a mercinary captain of some Gnoll Longbowmen with four other flind Lt., {Thinking...} Hrook'Oor (Howls-with-throat), Nargrotarg (Black-cave-of-stone), Raag-Kraar (Render-of-hides), and Gr'Kroth (Crushes-skulls-with-anger). The jist of the article is that every gnoll tribe has a totem animal they mist hunt and kill to attain adulthood, with tribes whose totems are things like humans or elves are highly regarded. "Flind" is a modification of a gnollish word for "Canibal" because their totem animal is the gnoll (highest of all, of course).

Nish Lahss is a character based on Beyond Countless Doorways by M.Cook, an indep d20 book. 'The Lizard Kingdoms' are a world where dinosaurs and reptililian races never faltered in dominanace. Mammilian races are mostly primitive goblins, gully dwarves, and pigmy humans who hunt at night and hide during the day. Lizard Folk, Dire Kobolds, Firenewts, etc. have thriving societies that trade with formian hives. The Trogs, adapted to cool caves and mountains, are the newest rising power, refining metals and contoling trade through the mountain passes. As far as the smell goes, I figured Trogs have amazing control of their scent glands, just as they do of their camoflage. A trog could smell like roses if she was hiding in a rose bush. It just so happens that they've learned that if they smell like burning garbage, sh*t, and ripe carrion, humanoids tend to become incapacitated, and use that to their advantage in battle.
