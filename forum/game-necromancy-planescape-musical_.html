Target was ECL 7, but creativity is encouraged over crunch.  Equipment should be practical with a few very specific wonderous items to help a character along.  I guess just try and tailor items to character.  Once again, fluff over crunch.

 

For instance, Stitch the Xaositect juggler has a hat of holding (basicly five bags of holding stitched together) containing various acts and costumes.  He carries a rapier, but usually in the hat.

Try to keep to the rule of threes, i.e. go for three very useful and appropriate items, and three minor ones, like shivs and potions.  Any shivs are considered to be "Keen Blades", +1 weapons of Sigilan make, unless the character concept calls for something else.

  Since the 'Yanki don't

 

Since the 'Yanki don't tend to get mixed up in Faction politics, the political state of Sigil doesn't come into play much in BoG'r.  This game, however, has the potential for some PCs to belong to a faction.

In my Planescape campaign, I play post-faction war with a little twist.  The Factions have largely returned to Sigil.  They now comprise about half of the posts that exist in their various city functions.  So about half the judges and clerks in the Courts are Guvners, about half the guards in the Prison are either Sodkillers or Sons of Mercy.  Etc.

The fact that Factotums haven't been mazed en masse leads most to believe the Lady's Edict was a warning, and you only had to give up your Faction for a day or even a moment when fear led many to flee the Faction War.  The Faction War itself is considered VERY poor taste to even mention aloud in public.

The government of Sigil consists on the street level with Guilds, Sects, powerful temples, and representatives of many of Sigil's unique and often racially divided districts.  These are followed by Ward representatives and the mechanics of government; the Courts, The Hall of Records, The Prison, and the Hall of Speakers.  The third tier is the Sigilan Advisory Council.  There are nine seats on the inner council, 27 (three to the third power) in the middle council and 729 (nine to the third power) in the outer council.  A Ward election is held every third year in a particular ward.  The Wards directly opposite Sigil's ring from each other vote, and it rotates around the ring every year.  The six Ward seats are three years each.  The three Sigil-wide seats are up every nine years, always in a year when the Lady's Ward and the Clerk's Ward vote.

Which reminds me ... combat.  It might not happen at all in the game, but it is set in Sigil, where anything can happen.  Unlike many other large cities throughout the planes, Sigil has absolutely no prohabition against open weaponry in the streets.  Thus the only people who do not walk around armed with something for their personal protection are either those who can afford bodyguards or those too destitute to own a blade.  The most popular class of weapon is of course the shiv, any blade from dagger to longsword, but tending toward the shorter end of the spectrum.  Due to Sigilan culture, to APPEAR unarmed is desirable.  A you know what the basher with a greatsword is going to reach for when civility breaks down, but a hidden shiv can come from anywhere.
