'Iavas' wrote:
So... when exactly did they get actual swords for arms... is that a 3e thing? I thought they were just guys with beaklike noses and wings instead of arms. And if they were, originally, just that, then why were they called Sword Archons. Am I missing something?

They were originally, in the 1e Manual of the Planes, described as humanoids with wings and the heads of cats. They had arms, and used them to carry swords as weapons, and acted as the weapon arms of the higher-ranking celestials.

They were illustrated this way in the 2e Monstrous Compendium Outer Planes Appendix.

Tony DiTerlizzi drew them a completely different way, humanoids with beaklike noses and no arms. I don't know why - probably it seemed like a good idea at the time. Bizarrely, they still had a bite attack, which made sense when they had cat heads but's just weird when their mouths look human.

In the 3.5 edition Book of Exalted Deeds, they were reimagined a second time as winged humanoids with fiery swords as arms. I like this version the best.
