Well, I always figured slavery is legal in Sigil, but the legality and existance of the institution as being much more complex than, say in the Roman Empire or even the colonial southern US.

Owning slaves is legal, buying and selling them is legal as a contract between the owners, but taking slaves or re-capturing escapees is considered assault and kidnapping. If a slave-owner has legal, official, concrete evidence of ownership from a large, stable nation, than they might get off with just 'disturbing the peace' which is likely to be a fine nearly as large as the value of the slave. The other problem for the slaver is the 'official' part. Prime-world nations the courts haven't heard of are no good, and Sigil itself does not officially regulate a slave trade or order certificates of ownership.

It all adds up to this for the slaver: Don't get caught. Don't let 'em get away. Get 'em out of Sigil quick. Or, you could register your slaves with the Baatezu, their contracts are iron-clad (literally?).

Econonic slavery is much more common. For example, the 'workers' in the Hive Brothel are technicly not slaves, as they are given a very small amount of jink from their pimps. Too little to get very far, of course, if they left.

The Sandstone District: This area has well-made sandstone buildings and walled courtyards.
