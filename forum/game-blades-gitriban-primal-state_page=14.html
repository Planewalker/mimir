Gy-Nath of Ironrock locks eyes with Jhank'kor the Dark. The warlock feels first the giph's emotions, an utter calm and focus of purpose, then he hears Gy's voice in his head.

"Jhank'kor, hear me, heed me. I have no doubt whatsoever that you speak truth, chal, or at least what you believe to be the truth. But that's the rub, isn't it? For what it's worth, I am not absolutely sure of your thralldom, for I saw you slay a ghaik in the Heart of the Void. Still, you bear several marks of a thrall, Jhank'kor, and not just a physical one. You are obsessed with ferreting out traitors, which may be a subconcious implant to make sure you remain one yourself. You have hidden your mark from others, purposefuly I wonder? Or were you programed to hide it? You have poor control of your emotional state, as I noticed from the first.

From where I stand there are four possibilities; First, that you are indeed a thrall of the ghaik, whether you realize it or not. If this is the case the safest thing would be to slay you right now, but I am not sure enough of this at the moment to take the life of a brother.

Second, that the ghaik tried to brainwash you but did not complete the job. This seems likely to me, but we will never get to the truth unless you calm down and start trusting me.

Third, that you are not brainwashed at all, just marked. If this is the case then you are not being influenced by the flayers, but are instead mentally unbalanced in your own right. This too makes sense if you went through what it sounds like you have, for the mark goes deep, I know.

Lastly, and the most disturbing possibility, is that you are completely enthralled, but the ghaik saw fit not to avert you from slaying their kind. If this is the case it turns what I know of the enthralled, and indeed of the ghaik, on it's head.

Hear me, Jhank'kor, heed me. Let me bandage your wrist. I must do so in a manner that keeps you from casting most spells. By taking the blade of your power, I pledge my blades to you. You shall not come to harm while my hands can hold a blade, nor while the blade of my mind remains sharp. Should you turn false my blades will meet yours. Choose."

The chal's eyes bulge at the motion of surrendering himself to the hilt of another. He hesitates as the anxiety presses against the invisible notion of enclosing walls. It is like when we are told to blindfold ourselves and follow another's voice, incapable of direction on our own. So, too, came this decision amid the unseen fear to Jhank'kor, proud warmage of the Lich Queen, member of her Onyx Band and zealous vassal. But, so too, came the realization he had a comrade who would finally support him. Blowing his nigh illimitable ire out in a long, ice-laced sigh, Jhank'kor presents his hands forward for the warrior to bind the wounds as he must. Utter calm now present in his pure black gaze, the chal says quietly, "I hope to one day be free of this, Gy'nath...though it fed me their thoughts, from which I could better attune by spells against them, I would rather have grown and died clueless--and thus unempowered--than to see so many slaughtered..."

He pauses to keep his all-too-eager rage from resurrecting in the ashes of sorrow. Glancing toward his scorched companion, the warlock says, "So, he deceived us, Daraz...I'm sorry, friend. Let this be our only taste of the Nine, shall we?" He face sprouts a feeble attempt at an honest grin, though decades of hatred tug it toward the form of a grimace than a kindly smile.
