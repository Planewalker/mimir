Mr. Smith?

Duke Charper's voice appears in Ohalian's mind again.

We have some really interesting results here. This Charknathrol... well, there's two things. First - ever heard of that musician, Jaimee Dark? Quite a sick man, if you ask me, and wanted by the police. He robs dead bodies from hospitals, and with a special technical equipment, uses their voices as instruments - that is, he makes dead people sing.

The interesting part is that those, well, dead voices lack lots of the normal vibration of a living voice, they sound hollow and... well, dead. One of the sound engineers surely could explain it better. Anyway, this Charknathrol has very similar results. Put short, you're speaking to a dead man there, if you look at the voice data.

Second thing, and this is really weird... his voice changes our data.

Duke makes a pause, taking a deep breath.

I really have no technical explanation for it yet. The moment we record Charknathrol's voice, the record is exactly what we hear. But when we listen to the records, things have changed. The words are the same, but there are small details that everyone agrees were different when we listened to the original. And the amazing thing is, we've tried a variety of recording instruments, even going down to simple tape recorders, and it's all the same. Really, I've never seen anything like that.
