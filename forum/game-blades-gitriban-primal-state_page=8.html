Daraz walks toward the Grand Bazaar, switching identities often when nobody is around. He peruses a few travelers' inns until he finds one not very crowded, with a pick of clueless patrons. He chooses a pair of young primes, apprentice mages fleeing their world's overlord, or some similar sob story, but obviously of noble lineage by the cut of the young man's jib and the sparklies that adorn his paramour's gown and locks. Daraz plays the part of a fellow traveler, a merchant (of sorts), one more knowlegable of the planes. He is sure to start off with some very sagely advice (and when he rolls the pair, they won't be able to say he didn't warn them to trust not in appearances). Their names are Ian and Elly.

The pair are deep in their cups with sorrow for their homeland when Daraz finds them, and a small investment to the bar produces a combination of liquors. The clueless are fascinated by the Arborian Honeydew, Bitopian Brandy, and Githzerai Sck'chaak, and after a couple of shots Archeronian Rust-Swag, they are well-primed (as it were).

Daraz manages to convince the pair that he knows the perfect magic and gem shop that will give them the best price for the gear they must pawn, because of course he knows the owner personally, and the old man has a soft spot for refugees, especially disposessed nobility. This shop is near the edge of Sigil.

Of course, Daraz knows that street traffic slacks off near the edges of Sigil's ring. Few want to live with their back walls to the void atop the spire. Daraz leads them, and the loot they took from the Mages' Accademy when they fled, to a secluded spot off of Magician's Street (and so not too far from Git'riban).

Unfortunately, Daraz chooses his ambush terrain too well. Just as he approaches the spot he had chosen, he notices they are being followed. Daraz shows no sign of noticing, but he quickly counts three tieflings getting set up to jump them. The primes are the perfect blend of naieve, drunk, oblivious, and rich. Daraz is going to have some competition for these two...

Prologue to the Next Scene

Haven Port Inn

The Inn in the Heart of the Void has existed for a long time, longer than it has borne it's present name. The story of how the current owner arrived is well known throughout Git'riban, partially because he wants it to be well known.

Some turns ago, a group of spelljamming gith pirates made the haul of hauls. Details about the hoard are sketchy, but it most certainly contained major amounts of magic that the pirates could not use themselves. One wily pirate, the Bosun of the ship, knew a secret the others did not. The Bosun knew of Sigil, and of a portal leading there. He managed to convince the captain that the best price for the booty was to be had at a particular port. Of course, by the time the ship arrived, only the Bosun and the Cook still lived.

Bosun and Cookie settled down to a new, comfortable life in Git'riban. "Convincing" the former owner of the Inn to leave, they renamed the place Haven Port. The couple has at least eight children, who help Bosun and Cookie run the Inn. Both Bosun and Cookie are older, but still active. Their youngest child is in her 20's.

One feature of the Inn building that predates Haven Port, and indeed all of Git'riban, are the arcane pillars within. Undispellable magical symbols adorn a series of pillars in the outer walls, visible from within the building. The spells prevent all scrying and teleportation, both magical and psionic, from penetrating the Inn.
