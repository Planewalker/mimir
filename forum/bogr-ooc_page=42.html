She can chalk it up to

She can chalk it up to "Well I AM the newest member of the group." but if you want to play her sociopatic personality, it should be more like.  "Oh no you didn't!  We'll see a*****e, not today, maybe not tomorrow or the next day, but I will pay you back eventually."

Yes, the DM is stirring up intra-party drama, again. 

But in any case, also notice that Ii was the only one who got a rank, or even any indication of the area of skill, in that whole introduction.  This is honor+ for Ii at the slight expense of the newcomer, because although Gy-Nath offers the Git'ribanis' service, Kirath really has no idea what capacity to use them in, save 'The Knight' who is sure to be a deadly physical combatant.  This also forces Kirath to rely on the band's command structure, relaying orders through Gy-Nath (whose rank remains unstated) to the rest.

Basicly, I see the introduction as many layered.  In all you can see it this way.

To Ii: net honor minus.  If she were still backed by Vlaakith, Ii as a knight would have been introduced before Gy introduced himself.  Instead, she is put in the place of a mere githwarrior.  He softens it by refering her title and no one else's, but still ...

To Kirath: net honor plus.  He called the raid "yours", but he obviously remains in command of his own group.

To K'tch:  Big plus.  K'tch is put in the place of sarth.  In fact, he isn't making any attempt to poker-face his pride, and has a big, sharp-toothed grin.

To Everyone else:  Nothing, but an issue might arrise if the newcomer starts issuing ill-advised orders directly to a party-member.  Gy would have to step in at that point, because he made the offer, not the PC.
