Given our layout editor's time constraints decided to move the Prisine Conversions and decided to save the Modron art from the first Issue. This gives us a bit of slack for Issue 2, given that time is obviously going to a bottle neck for all of us.

I also want to lower the page count to about 15-18 pages, allowing us to hopefully give Mogget time for layout.

Anyway, as always none of this is written in stone. Feel free to suggest changes in methodology and apologies for making some "executive" decisions here.

Edit: Mogget made a good point, that splitting up the Killing Lady article might be more annoying that interesting. Since he's the layout editor, I figure if he doesn't mind taking on the extra pages it's cool. So we'll set that article to wrap up in Issue 2 which is good since I have a second Lady Picture for that.

Still going to try to keep these shorter that 20 pages, at least until we can get art for articles at a faster rate which ideally means more artists 'cause I know how hard y'all work!

Haha no photoshop is definitely not ideal software for creating pdfs. I just meant that if we get into a bind and can't get ahold of anyone else, I can fandangle something together after all.

Two columns vs three columns isn't really something I have a meaningful opinion on. I do feel that the issue with the three column layout was that we used it in conjunction with a very long and thin page, which resulted in a lot of scrolling down, then up, then down again, then back up again.

Regardless of what else we do, we probably want page dimensions where most people can comfortably see a whole page on the screen at the same time. If this isn't a thing that is happening, then minimizing columns is what we want.

Sciborg, I think those are great ideas but we will have to be very careful about what welre endorsing in the zine. This isn't an issue where it concerns hilighting things that are happening on planewalker, but as we start showcasing off-site items we have to make sure we're linking to the source of the media and not a mirror or an image sharing website; we should be especially wary of tumblr accounts, for example.
