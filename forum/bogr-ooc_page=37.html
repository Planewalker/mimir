It seems the game generally has followed this trend for a long while, and I like how it keeps momentum and makes combat run faster in a play-by-post game.

However, I'll point out that there's a downside in connecting character initiative with player posting frequency. Characters whose strength lies in being first to act will have lost it, which is fairly important for rogue and assassin-like characters and critters. I'm pretty much all for keeping this system because it makes the game go faster, but I'll admit that Du'minh's Improved Initiative feat feels like a wasted investment.

About 6 hours game-time since the duel with Gy-Nath.

BTW, note the spelling, it is significant in the sense that as a foot-soldier his name was probably just Gy, or Nath, or some derivation thereof (actually, An'nath, if you must know). He would have changed his name to the two-part "Gy-Nath" to denote a higher status when he bacame a commanding officer. Githyanki names don't really follow any rules like this, but githyanki do sometimes change or modify their names as their life circumstances change. In general, the longer the name, the higher status the githyanki, though it is not a firm rule. In this vein, I did not think it mere coincidence that Ii'jyka'vaar, a knight (a high status position, during Vlaakith's reign anyway) has the longest name among the PCs. I figure as a hatchling her name could have been "Jyka" or something.

Other examples:  Gy-Nath's supreme leader was named as "Ali-Gorthifsha-Na'Khaal", a rather long name.  The NPC Telas-Niah is about the same level of power/ status (or would be if she wasn't an exile) as Gy-Nath.
