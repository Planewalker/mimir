When the "rubble pile" pulls itself up to a towereing 9' height, and six long tenticles lash outward, Jhank'kor knows he faces an unusual foe he's heard of but has never faced: A Urophion.

Jhank'kor knows how the illithid breed, starting off their lives as slimy tadpoles until allowed to burrow into the skull of a humanoid creature. No known examples exist of successful ceremorphisis in non-humanoid creatures, except one. Somehow, a monsterous cave preditor known as a 'roper' proves a viable host species. The result, the urophion, combines the mind and psionic powers of an illithid with the physical prowres of a roper. They are very rare, and used almost exclusively as sentries. Their lot is solitude and dreary service to the elder brains, being a creature barely considered illithid by the illithid.

A rare smile came upon Duminhs face at the sight of his Gitriban home.

The faade displays a robust building, with seemingly nothing in the way of windows and the like, but plenty of arrow-slits and concealed openings that provide vantage points for defending the house. When passers-bys get closer, it quickly becomes apparent that the residence is built as a military installation, and its surroundings tailored to defend it. Spikes make the walls hazardous to climb, and plenty of undergrowth lines the walls, making it nearly impossible to sneak around outside the perimeters. For those of keen sight, it also appears there are numerous tripwires along the ground, except for the path leading up to the entranceway. At closer inspection one might also spot the faint trace of magical wards indicating abjurations that protect the premises, and the presence of lead blocking off divinations. Of course, cunning observers will quickly realize that these barely visible defences are meant to scare off potential trespassers, while the really fatal traps and wards are much better hidden from view.
On the backside of the building there is a courtyard, but the yard is hidden from street-level by walls overgrown with razorvine.

As the trio entered and helped Kdar to rest, Duminh assumed a more calm attitude, and ensured that all aids in the house that could speed up his recovery, where being employed.

Give me a moment, brother, then we can speak.

Duminh quickly surveys the security measures in place throughout the house, checking all the traps and wards to see if anything has been tampered with. Assuming he finds everything to be in order, he introduces Ardru to the central chambers on the ground floor, a combination of common room and training chambers a hint of Duminhs lifestyle. The walls of these chambers are lined with a variety of mundane weapons and armor, along with long hanging scrolls that recount the passages of the legends of Gith. Everything shines of a warrior who loves his craft and prides himself on his heritage.
On a table nearby lies a stack of recent issues of the prestigious newsrag The Tempus Sigilan, as well as books on a variety of incantations and spells, although they dont seem to be written from the perspective of a mage.

Welcome to my home, Ardru. There is whetstone and any tools you may need to maintain your gear, and further down you will find water and sanitation to freshen up and patch wounds. I will be with you in a moment.

Returning to Kdar in his sleeping chambers on the second floor, Duminh closes the door behind him and pulls up a chair next to his warband commander.

Though the hour is grim, it is good to see you, Kdar. We can speak freely now, and though you are in pain, I ask that you recount what intelligence you have for me as best you can. I have information for you as well: Sight of the Three is dead, slain by this group I have been fighting with today. What can you tell me of the attack, Telas-Niah, and Grazz?
