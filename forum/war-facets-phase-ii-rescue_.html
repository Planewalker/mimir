War of the Facets -- Phase II: Rescue

The City of Glass, The Dancing Mermaid 8:50 AM, Day 4 of the War of the Facets

Thalivan treated you all to a lavish breakfast before sending you off.  The fare at the Mermaid was delicious, and Thalivan, being an arcane, spared little expense in taking care of his employees.  After all, a well-treated employee performed better, and Thalivan could always cut the cord if he felt his workers were unsatisfactory.  The repast consisted mostly of seafood, some you recognized and others you didn't.  There was some familiar dry-land food, too.  Some bacon, eggs, rolls and cheese accompanied the exotic local fare.

Over breakfast, the arcane explained the task.  Business as usual, your tall, blue-skinned employer spared little time in giving you the assignment.  You were to travel to a city called Aremshah on the Plane of Air, a popular trading hub noted for its portals to several planes, including a large caravan portal to Sigil.  Once there, you were to go to the Glowing Gem after dinner.  A water genasi named Ghadir spent his evenings at the Gem, which catered primarily to genasi of all types.  If anyone knew the locations of Salt portals in the City of Glass, it'd be him.  And if he was reluctant to talk, Thalivan said to use his name, and possibly a little garnish, and Ghadir would be glad to help.  He gave you every assurance that he would talk to the local guard, as well as research the origin of the symbol.  And so, armed with directions, information, and a person to find, you set off for the Glowing Gem on the Plane of Air.



 

Aremshah, The Glowing Gem 9:30 PM, Day 4 of the War of the Facets

You arrived at the city of Aremshah in the morning and had the entire day to spend doing whatever you needed or wanted to do.  The city was a mangificent metropolis with beautiful spires of glass and crystal.  In one section of the city, the Temple of Akadi dominated the skyline. Its glassy, transparent curves and magnificent airy shapes reached up into the endless blue sky above.  The Palace of the Caliph towered over another section of the city, its slender towers and minarets gleaming like a series of stars and gems in the sky.  It was a majestic sight, a testament to the wealth and prestige of the Caliph.  Interestingly, the current Caliph, As'ad, was no djinn at all, but the air genasi son of a djinn and a mortal woman.  As'ad held a reputation as a powerful druid, however, and a prominent master of elemental energy, and had gained the respect of his people despite not being a full-blood djinn.


 



Chances were that you'd never get the opportunity to see As'ad, though.  Your travels were to take you away from the palace and the surrounding neighborhoods and toward the seedier areas of town. So, later that evening after spending time wandering the city and doing a bit of shopping and sightseeing, you all regrouped and made your way to the Glowing Gem.



Though not properly in the slums, the Glowing Gem sat close to the less-prestigious areas of Aremshah.  The Gem saw its fair share of unsavory types as well as a number of more upstanding citizens.  Most of the patrons were indeed genasi of all different shapes and colors.  The interior was decorated with colorful glass lanterns hanging from the ceiling, all stained to resemble glowing gemstones that gave the bar its name.  Entrance seemed to be either at a premium or with a modest cover charge, judging by the people that stopped to talk to the bouncers at the front door.  Three fire genasi did most of the talking, but one creature stood off to the side, looking a little out of place...



 



Welcome to Phase II!  This one will be a bit short.  At any rate, you have a most of a full day to do whatever you might want to do.  Shopping, wandering, etc.  If you buy things, let me know what you pick up.  Aremshah is a trade hub, so you can find just about anything you need to get.  Check the OOC for your XP awards, among other things.

It was not every day that a

It was not every day that a group of a water genasi, a warforged, an elderly human, a human-looking girl, and what appeared to be a dwarf showed up outside of the Glowing Gem. It was, frankly, a joke in sore need of a punchline. They looked, in fact, like adventurers. Who, as many knew to their grief, were first-class troublemakers. The trio of fire genasi who had been handling most of the duties before exchanged glances, then as a group ceded this pack of potential problems to the fourth member of the crew.

The seeming-boss of the crew heaved a sigh, noted the page number of his book, and tucked it under his arm. The best word for the creature was... intimidating. One could see why he was a bouncer. Even a very inebriated troublemaker would have thoughts of mortality when being loomed over by a seven-and-a-half foot tall hyena-headed monstrosity with sharp claws, sharp teeth, and a sharp knife.

 "No begging." The creature's voice was gravelly, and not very far off from a snarl, with a peculiar accent. "Pay fee."

 "Cover fee twenty gold per person, pay Drachmir." The creature said slowly, as if reciting something drilled into it. The hyena-man poked a single, clawed thumb into his muscled chest, indicating that it was the selfsame Drachmir. He looked you over for another moment. "No. Thirty-five gold. Look like trouble."

The ruse seemed to have

The ruse seemed to have worked and despite the gnoll's ferocious look, he shaped up very quickly under pressure from the "Epicurean Society."  And so, you all made it inside without mishap.  Warm colors filled the Gem's interior and the dull roar of conversation greeted your ears as you passed through the threshold into the bar.  Nearly all of the patrons were genasi and their presence filled the room with a veritable array of colors.  At one table, a wiry, grey-skinned genasi dressed in a light grey vest and loose pants idly blew smoke rings into the air while two bare-chested, white-skinned genasi sat nearby.  They must've been ice genasi -- their appearances strongly resembled K'zink, but less drenched from ambient humidity and condensation.  Over there, a sandy brown-skinned genasi man dressed in azure robes cheerfully engaged a dark-skinned woman in conversation.  It seemed innocent enough until you noticed the twin shamshirs underneath the male genasi's outer robe.  A man of average build with vibrant indigo skin and a bright shock of red hair tended the bar.  A faint, warm blue glow seemed to cling to him even amid the light of the gemstone lanterns and his eyes glowed like red suns.  He served up the sparkling starfall with expert ease and soon Tamara found herself with a rich, dark drink that shimmered with motes of silvery light.  (DM's note: Cost is negligible, no need to mark it.)

Coal noticed during his scrutiny of the lamps that a couple of lanterns actually held pure quasi-elemental radiance rather than fire.  Most of these lamps were closer to or trained toward the stage that dominated the back half of the room.  A bit of applause fluttered through the room as the most recent act -- a band of earth genasi drummers -- made its exit.  The applause died down and the regular murmuring continued.  You all scanned the room, but weren't sure where to find Ghadir.  Different types of genasi were distinct enough that even in a room full of them, a body could pick one type from the other.  But of the relatively few water genasi in the bar right now, most were female.  Of the men, none of them looked like gatecrashers or well-lanned planewalkers, but who could really say?

As you perused the bar, the stage curtains raised and a new act was announced.

"And now, tonight's top performance, the lovely Lilac!"

Two air genasi men, dressed in little more than silken scarves and sashes that wrapped around them and floated in their wake like wind trails walked out on stage and knelt down, one on each end.  They each produced some kind of unusual woodwind instrument and began playing a sinuous, airy melody.  The sound of conversation quieted some as many patrons stopped to watch the new act.  Then, the star of the show emerged.

Put simply, she was utterly radiant, in every sense of the word.  She had rich, vivid purple skin and long hair, as bright and yellow as a Prime sun, tied into a high ponytail that cascaded down her back like a waterfall of light.  She wore layers of diaphanous clothing that shimmered and scintillated as it caught the light when she moved.  She danced and swayed with incredible, unearthly grace, such that she might be an angel.  When she shook her hips, tiny bells jingled in time with the air genasis' music.  You realized quickly why Drachmir said Lady Lilac was the best dancer in the house.  As you watched, you noticed the expert execution.  It wasn't just a pretty woman dancing, it was a carefully constructed display of color, light, and sound all embodied by Lilac's own music.

Perhaps this performance would be worth an extra star or two to the Sigil Epicurean Society.

All righty, there's no shortage of people to talk to here.  You can't talk to Lilac obviously since she's dancing.  But you can talk to the barkeep or pick a random genasi out of the crowd.  There're lots more than what I've listed here, including serving boys and girls of various types and colors.  So whoever you talk to, we'll work with it!
