On the Nature of Soul

So, just for fun, let us discuss the possible nature of petitioners. Sure, we know that when mortals die, their souls go to the Outer Planes and become petitioners: weak but physical representations of their mortal beliefs with no memories and a burning desire to merge with their new home. Which part of their mortal selves do petitioners actually retain? How are ghosts created, and can a person's ghost and petitioner exist simultaneously. How about necromantically reanimating their corpse - does that affect their petitioner at all?

I propose something along the lines of the Egyptian idea of the soul. After all, the Egyptian pantheon is as prominant in Planescape as any other, and since they are more ancient than most pantheons, there's no reason why their idea couldn't have hit closer to the 'truth'. Continuing the idea of a pentaptych soul, let is define it in Planescape terms. I will name the different parts by their Egyptian names initially bolded and subsequently italicized, with possible Socratic/Platonic parallels in {curly brackets}.

*******

We are all familiar with the Ha, or material body. It is given life by the composite entity known as the soul. After the death of the Ha, certain parts of the soul travel to the Outer Planes and give an afterlife to the petitioner's body, or Akh. Since the Akh is made of bits of the Outer Plane that will become the petitioner’s new home, it will be subject to the wills of powers and the nature of the plane itself, being reshaped thereby. The five different parts of the soul are described below.

Ka {Thymos}:
This is the life-force that is responsible for animating a being’s Ha, the Akh, and any other bit that needs moving. After the death of the Ha, it will travel to the Outer Planes and naturally merge with the Ba, Ren, and some surrounding planar matter, reshaping the latter into a fitting Akh, thus creating a new petitioner. If the petitioner is turned into an outsider, the Ka will continue powering that outsider until the end of its existence. After the death or absorption of the petitioner or outsider that the Ka animates in the afterlife, it will return to the source of all life (Positive Energy Plane?) and be reborn into another mortal.

Ba {Logos}:
This is the personality that defines a being's alignment and nature. It is created as a blank neutral during a mortal's birth and continues to evolve and change over the course of its life and afterlife. After the death of the Ha, it will travel to the Outer Planes and naturally merge with the Ka, Ren and Akh, thus creating a new petitioner. If the petitioner is turned into an outsider, the Ba will continue being shaped by that outsider's decisions until the end of its existence. After the death or absorption of the petitioner or outsider that the Ba inhabits in the afterlife, it is absorbed into the plane that most closely matches its disposition at that point (or, alternately, the plane on which it is destroyed), merging with it. These unique additions to a plane's philosophy gradually change that plane's nature. If too many contradicting Ba's collect in any single plane, they have the potential to divide it into two or more new planes, each with subtly different natures.

Ren {Eros}:
This is the true name, which drives a being to action. It is established during a mortal’s birth and continues guiding and evolving with it over the course of its life and afterlife. The Ren determines the needs of a being’s Ha/Akh and the intent of its Ib, guiding the Ka to fulfill those according to the nature of its Ba. This guidance can be hijacked by properly speaking the verbal equivalent of the Ren, allowing another being to take some control over the actions of its owner. After the death of the Ha, it will travel to the Outer Planes and naturally merge with the Ka, Ba and Akh, thus creating a new petitioner. If the petitioner is turned into an outsider, the Ren will continue guiding and evolving with that outsider until the end of its existence. After the death or absorption of the petitioner or outsider that the Ren guides in the afterlife, it either dissolves completely or disappears beyond the reach of all recorded knowledge (Ordial?).

Ib:
This is the mental processes of a being. A new one is created every time a Ka animates an appropriate matter, either the Ha or the Akh, so the mortal’s Ib is different from that of his/her petitioner. The Ib processes logic and stores memories using the brain (or similar organ) until the death of the current body, at which point it disperses into the Astral and fueling the winds of that plane. The exact fate of the Ib after such a dispersion is unknown, but some suggest that the ideas so collected on the Astral influence the morphogenic fields of the Outer, and perhaps even more material, planes. An Ib can also be taken away by certain spells and the by the touch of Styx water, at which point a new one begins to develop if the rest of the being is still alive. Those stolen by the Styx are thought to end up in the ice of Ocanthus.

Sheut:
This is the a mortality of a being, sometimes called its shadow. While regular shadows are cast by most, if not all, things, the Sheut is only known to be a part of mortal creatures. Responsible for the death and subsequent processes in a being’s life, the Sheut is still the least understood part of the mortal soul. It is thought to be associated with the Negative Energy Plane as well as the Demiplane of Shadow. Whether the denizens of either plane are or once were Sheuts is a popular topic of debate among philosophers. The beliefs of certain cultures that vampires and other undead do not cast shadows or reflections is based on those undead beings’ lack of mortality, or Sheut. Whether petitioners and outsiders, which, while not strictly mortal, can be destroyed, have a Sheut and whether it is the same as that of their mortal forms is unknown, since nobody has been able to track the fate of the Sheut for any length of time after a mortal’s death. There is some suggestion that the ‘vestiges’ that practitioners of pact magic bind themselves to are actually the Sheuts of deceased creatures. The Sheut cannot be separated from a living being by any known means. Chant is that some powerful night hag once managed to discover the secret to such a process and created a powerful, almost godlike, being from a nameless human’s separate Sheut, but this probably just screed.

*******

Mindless undead are simply deceased Has animated by magic and the necromancer’s own Ka.
Intelligent corporeal undead are believed to be dead Has that somehow retain their own Ka, Ren, and Ib while lacking their original Ba and Sheut. A Ba lost in such a transformation is absorbed by the plane on which its petitioner would have appeared after death, but the process is slow, since for the plane it is akin to a human digesting raw oats instead of preparing them first.
Incorporeal undead are varied. Ghosts are the Ib of a deceased creature that got stuck in the Ethereal on the way to the Astral and can exist independently of that creature’s petitioner. Destroying a ghost or “putting it to peace” is simply the release of the Ib into the Astral.
Larvae are petitioners whose evil Ba is somehow altered by arcane processes known only to Night Hags and high-up Yugoloths. Such unlucky petitioner’s new Ib is completely destroyed and left blank until later replacement and/or development while the Akh is twisted to reflect their flawed natures. Some believe that there are even two types of larvae, with the Yugoloths depositing waste Ba into their souls while the Night Hags steal or devour all but the purely evil Ba the soul might still contain.

So, the main problem everybody seems to be having is primarily with ghosts. Any alternate suggestions for incorporating the misty ones into the pentaptych souls sytem (PSS™)? Keep in mind, ghosts seem to retain their memories and, to a certain degree, their personalities, so the Ib and, possibly, the Ba probably remain. Besides the fleshy bits, they have also lost their mortality (bye bye Sheut). Whether they still have (or need) the Ka and the Ren is completely up in the air. Given all that, I can see them being simply petitioners that have been held back from forming an Akh on the planes by retaining their Ib.

That leaves us with sorting out spectres, wraiths, and other ghost-like meanies.
