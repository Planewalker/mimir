-MotM was a high-level telepath, specializing in domination and extracting information from unwilling minds. She was also an expert in planting post-hypnotic suggestions.

-She would have been a scholar of Mind Flayer techniques of enthrallment, their writing, and psi-tech such as mind-marking and crystal resonance.

>MotM is known to have posessed the High Blade of Mulmaster and dominate or plant suggestions in the rest of the rulers of that Graith city. The Mulmasterites are friendly to the githyanki even to this day.
>MotM had also spent a great deal of time traveling Faerun before the Incursion, planting spies. She had hypnotic suggestions in the minds of hundreds, if not thousands of Graith, who would aid the githyanki when the time came. It was partially for this reason that the githyanki generally didn't kill any graith who wielded no weapons. That farmer is not only useful, but they might even be a dominated spy for their cause. It is also suspected by some that this was the reason the Dales andcertain other cities and lands were off-limits by royal decree.
>MotM was essential for the founding of Kurogolomsh. The name is a bastardization "wretched super-lair", and was an area of the middledark under the Earthspur Mountains infested with destrachans (see MM). The githyanki attacked, and MotM possessed the largest, meanest one of the aberrations. She then used it to carve access tunnels to the surface and to other areas of the nearby underdark. Some had assumed MotM was in fact destroyed when a void bomb blasted Kurogolomsh, as she was last seen still 'riding' the monster. (Kirath was in a different part of the underdark, so knows only a little bit about this, or he wouldn't be asking)

To Az: first of all, welcome back! Hope all is well.

As for the sexuality discussion (heh heh) ...

Yes, perpetuation of strength and vitality in the species is a major component, but the same can be said of human attraction too. A strong, brave githwarrior will likely attract some interest in the same way a 'cute' or 'hot' member of the opposite gender will in our own society.

As for sex for pleasure, I could see it as being just as, if not MORE prevalent in githyanki society. Think about it, their society has an effective form of birth control: giving up the resulting egg to the varsh caste. Not only is this accepted by their society, but encouraged, so I'd think casual sex would be at least as accepted by githyanki as it is in liberal human cultures ( like our own modern western culture ), and far more than in conservative human cultures.

So, feelings of attachment might be socially suppressed, but physical attraction actually encouraged. This doesn't mean all githyanki are promiscuous, shallow, 'players'. But I do see githyanki sexuality as being similar to what we find in modern western culture, minus a few traditional hang-ups, and figuring that all those people who get married only to divorce wouldn't bother in githyanki society.

Of course, feel free to argue the point with me, moving the discussion to 'githyanki sexuality' in the rrakkma section of the forums.

My guess/opinion is similar to Azure's, but with the following additions/comments:

The entire subject is just considered less important overall than it is in most human cultures. When someone in modern Western culture turns on the radio, they're extremely likely to hear one love song after another. Most of the githyanki cultural energy is devoted toward warfare, the utter destruction of the Great Enemy, destruction of all githzerai, destruction/conquest of barbarians, etc.

While githyanki can indeed "fall in love" or feel emotional attachment, duty is expected to take precedence over love. (The tale of N'ach't is a good example.) Telas-Niah isn't only scandalous because her lover was a githzerai, but because (another githyanki might argue that) her sense of love overrode her sense of duty.

I'm guessing that a typical githyanki might be deeply disturbed by the idea of formal eugenics. Given, however, that the Revered Queen was actually trying to replace all githyanki with "perfect" duthka'giths (though most githyanki, of course, did not know this), I wonder what the half-dragons were taught, especially those who were closest to the Queen's inner circle? It's possible that they might spend more time consciously considering the subject of eugenics...
