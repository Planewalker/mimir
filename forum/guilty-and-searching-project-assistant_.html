What needs to be done by the Project Assistant (PA) is:

1. Looking out in the threads for questions that need to be solved.
If such a question comes up, there's the following options:
a) The question has already been solved in the project outlines, the PA only needs to give an answer referring to it.
Example: "I'd like to set up a rule saying Night Hags are really only giant squirrels with masks! Is that okay?" The answer would be, no, it's not okay, because the project outline says we don't mess up existing sources.
b) The question is solved by discussing/voting, the PA should then coordinate the discussions, possibly also start a new thread for it.
c) There's some reason why discussing this wouldn't be a good idea (e.g. the opinions are so different that a discussion would take years to solve), then we need a "decision from the top". The PA contacts me by PM and we decide it together.

2. If a topic gets stuck before it's really finished, the PA should try re-starting discussions, both by contacting writers and by posting new ideas and opinions himself.

3. The PA should get at least a rough overview of the new material posted in this forum, and see what fits the current project we're working on, as well as look out for how a posting should be changed to fit into our work.
Example: Someone posts an article about an amazing new location, but there's not a word about Hordlings or Hags in there. The PA tells the writer about it and gives ideas what could be changed to make the article fit into the project (e.g., "you could add something about Hags right there...").

4. The PA should be diplomatic and friendly in everything he does

Put short, the PA's task is to try keep things going here, and to make sure the project's theme and basic ideas are kept in the articles that are posted.

The requirements are, obviously, a good understanding of what the project's about, as well as mentioned "diplomatic understanding", but I think there's few people in here who don't have that.
