I just realized there was a smiley in the PM... wow... talk about missing the boat. Ok, so here's the nitty gritty for Drum's combat, just lemme know if all this works out:

-2/-2 penalties for 2 weapon fighting, +2 any attack with a longsword attk longsword (weapon focus, greater weapon focus), +2 base damage bonus (strength), +4 damage longsword (weapon specialization, greater weapon specialization) , longsword +3 forehand (additional +2d6 vs Lawful, additional 2d6 vs Good), longsword +5 offhand

So the +2 attack with any longsword (weapon focus feats) negates the -2/-2 to 0/0. Then the Base attack Bonus is +16/+5 (forehand), and 0/-5/-10 (offhand) (all the extra attacks with the offhand are due to Improved 2 weapon fighting, and greater 2 weapon fighting)..

Therefore,

Forehand with longsword +3 =
Attack: +19/+8
Damage: 1d8+9 = 1d8 +2(str) +4(grtr wpn specialization) +3(magic)
**an additional 2d6 vs Lawful/or Good creatures; an additional 4d6 vs Lawful Good.

Offhand with Longsword +5 =
Attack: +5/0/-5
Damage: 1d8+11 = 1d8 +2 (Str) + 4(grtr wpn specialization) +5(magic)

*18-20 x2 critical (Improved Critical: Longsword)

some rules about invisibility that I did not know before

Visually undetectable. An invisible creature gains a +2 bonus on attack rolls against sighted opponents, and ignores its opponents' Dexterity bonuses to AC (if any). (Invisibility has no effect against blinded or otherwise nonsighted creatures.) An invisible creature's location cannot be pinpointed by visual means, including darkvision. It has total concealment; even if an attacker correctly guesses the invisible creature's location, the attacker has a 50% miss chance in combat.
